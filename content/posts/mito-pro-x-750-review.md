## How Does It Work?
Red light therapy, also known as photobiomodulation, works by emitting specific wavelengths of light that penetrate the skin and stimulate cellular activity. You can learn more about the optimal [wavelength for red light therapy](/red-light-therapy/) in our detailed guide. This process encourages mitochondria, the powerhouses of our cells, to produce more adenosine triphosphate (ATP). ATP is the energy currency cells use to function, repair, and regenerate. The [red light therapy benefits](/red-light-therapy/) extend beyond just energy production; it has been found to aid in reducing inflammation, promoting wound healing, and improving skin health. Additionally, many users report enhanced mood and recovery from exercise-related fatigue. As research continues, the potential applications of this therapy in various health and wellness areas are becoming increasingly evident.
