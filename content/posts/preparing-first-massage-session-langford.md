                <h2>Aftercare: Maximizing Your Benefits</h2>
                <p class="mb-6">What you do after your massage is crucial for prolonging the benefits and managing any temporary soreness.</p>
                 <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                    <h3 class="!mt-0">The Golden Rule: Hydrate</h3>
                    <p>This is the most important step. Massage can release metabolic byproducts from your muscles, and drinking plenty of water helps your system flush them out, preventing headaches and sluggishness.</p>

                    <h3 class="mt-6">Managing Post-Massage Soreness (DOMS)</h3>
                    <p>It's normal to feel some muscle soreness a day or two after your first massage, similar to after a workout. This is called Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS) and it's a sign that positive change has occurred in your muscle tissues. To manage it:</p>
                     <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2 mt-4">
                        <li><strong>Rest:</strong> Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours.</li>
                        <li><strong>Take a Warm Bath:</strong> An Epsom salt bath is excellent for soothing sore muscles.</li>
                        <li><strong>Gentle Movement:</strong> A light walk or gentle stretching can prevent stiffness and promote circulation.</li>
