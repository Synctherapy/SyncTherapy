            <section id="what-is-armra">
                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">What Actually Is ARMRA?</h2>
                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md mt-6">
                    <p class="text-gray-700 mb-4">ARMRA is bovine colostrum — the first milk produced by cows after giving birth. It's rich in immunoglobulins (antibodies), lactoferrin, growth factors, and peptides that support immune function and gut barrier integrity.</p>
                    
                    <div class="bg-amber-50 border-l-4 border-amber-500 p-4 rounded-lg mb-6">
                        <h4 class="font-bold text-amber-800 mb-2">Important: ARMRA Is NOT a Probiotic</h4>
                        <p class="text-gray-700">This is the most common misconception. Probiotics contain live beneficial bacteria. Colostrum does not contain live bacteria — it's a compound that supports gut health through different mechanisms:</p>
                        <ul class="list-disc list-inside mt-3 space-y-2 text-gray-700">
                            <li><strong>Immunoglobulins:</strong> Antibodies that help neutralize pathogens</li>
                            <li><strong>Lactoferrin:</strong> Iron-binding protein with antimicrobial properties</li>
                            <li><strong>Growth factors:</strong> Support gut lining repair and integrity</li>
                            <li><strong>PRPs (Proline-rich polypeptides):</strong> Modulate immune response</li>
                        </ul>
                    </div>

                    <p class="text-gray-700 mb-4">ARMRA markets itself around immune support and "gut health," which creates confusion. The product is essentially a colostrum extract — similar to what you'd find in <a href="/best-leaky-gut-supplements/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">leaky gut supplements</a>, just rebranded with premium packaging and influencer marketing.</p>

                    <p class="text-gray-700">If you're looking specifically for probiotic support (populating your gut with beneficial bacteria), ARMRA won't deliver that. But if you've got a damaged gut lining or want immune support, colostrum has legitimate research behind it.</p>
                </div>
            </section>
