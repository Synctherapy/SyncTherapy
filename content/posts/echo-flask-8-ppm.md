            <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900"><i class="fas fa-flask text-accent mr-2"></i>What Does PPM Mean?</h2>
            <p class="text-gray-700 mt-3"><strong>PPM</strong> stands for "parts per million." In hydrogen water, it measures how many milligrams of dissolved molecular hydrogen (H2) exist per liter of water. At 8 ppm, the Echo Flask dissolves 8 milligrams of hydrogen into every liter of water. That's approximately 2.4 mg per 12 oz serving.</p>
            <p class="text-gray-700 mt-3">For context, most hydrogen water research uses concentrations between 0.5-1.6 ppm. The Echo Flask delivers well above that threshold, even on the 10-minute cycle.</p>
        </section>

            <p class="text-gray-700 mt-3">The Echo Flask uses SPE/PEM electrolysis with platinum-coated titanium electrodes. An electric current splits water molecules, the PEM membrane separates hydrogen from oxygen and byproducts, and pure molecular hydrogen dissolves into the water as nanobubbles. The sealed design pressurizes the water slightly, allowing more hydrogen to dissolve than open-top generators.</p>
