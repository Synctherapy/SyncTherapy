                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">The Most Effective Probiotic Strains for Acid Reflux</h2>
                <div class="space-y-6 bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                    <p>Not all probiotics are created equal when it comes to managing GERD. Research has shown that specific strains from the <strong>Lactobacillus</strong> and <strong>Bifidobacterium</strong> groups are the most promising for reducing symptoms and improving upper gastrointestinal health. </p>
                    <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Key Probiotic Strains to Look For:</h4>
                    <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-3">
                        <li><strong>Lactobacillus gasseri:</strong> Shown to reduce acid reflux symptoms by decreasing esophageal inflammation and improving how quickly the stomach empties.</li>
                        <li><strong>Bifidobacterium bifidum:</strong> May help by increasing the production of mucin (a protective mucus in the stomach) and speeding up gastric emptying to reduce reflux.</li>
                        <li><strong>Bifidobacterium lactis:</strong> Supports faster gastric motility, which can lower the frequency of acid regurgitation episodes.</li>
                        <li><strong>Lactobacillus rhamnosus:</strong> Particularly helpful for people taking proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), as it can reduce bacterial overgrowth sometimes linked to these medications.</li>
                        <li><strong>Lactobacillus plantarum:</strong> Associated with symptom improvement when combined with other strains in acid reflux patients.</li>
                        <li><strong>Lactobacillus reuteri:</strong> May provide aid in acid reflux cases that are linked to an H. pylori infection.</li>
                    </ul>
