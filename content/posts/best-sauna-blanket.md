<h2 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1726944528244">Why Choose an Infrared Sauna Blanket?</h2>
    <div id="1726944528245" class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small     margin-top_none  " section-id="1726944528245">
      <p>An infrared sauna blanket delivers far infrared heat directly to your body — unlike a traditional sauna that heats the air around you. This means you start sweating faster, use less energy, and can enjoy a session in any room of your home without installing a permanent sauna.</p>
<br><p>From a clinical perspective, I recommend infrared sauna blankets to clients for <strong>muscle recovery after workouts</strong>, <strong>stress and tension relief</strong>, <strong>improved sleep quality</strong>, and <strong>mood elevation</strong>. The deep penetrating heat increases blood flow and can help reduce inflammation — making it a useful complement to <a href="/red-light-therapy/">red light therapy</a> and other recovery tools.</p>
<br><p>Compared to a full infrared sauna cabin ($3,000–$8,000+), a quality sauna blanket costs $250–$999 and stores in a closet. For a complete breakdown of what infrared sauna therapy can do for you, read our guide on <a href="/sauna-blanket-benefits/">sauna blanket benefits</a>.</p>
    </div>
<h3 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1726944528249">Infrared Heat Benefits</h3>
    <div id="1726944528250" class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small     margin-top_none  " section-id="1726944528250">
      <p>Far infrared heat penetrates 1.5–3 inches below the skin, raising your core body temperature and triggering a deep sweat at lower air temperatures (typically 50–75°C vs. 80–100°C in a traditional sauna). Here's what the research and our hands-on testing show:</p>
<br><ul>
<li><strong>Detoxification</strong> — A 30–45 minute session can produce significant sweating, which may help flush out environmental toxins through the skin</li>
<li><strong>Muscle recovery</strong> — Increased blood flow delivers more oxygen to tired muscles, reducing soreness after exercise</li>
<li><strong>Stress reduction</strong> — The heat triggers endorphin release and activates the parasympathetic nervous system, helping you wind down</li>
<li><strong>Calorie burn</strong> — Brands claim 200–600 calories per session; realistically, expect a modest calorie burn equivalent to a light walk, mostly from water loss</li>
<li><strong>Better sleep</strong> — I personally use my sauna blanket 2–3 hours before bed and notice meaningfully deeper sleep on session nights</li>
</ul>
<br><p>For best results, start with 20-minute sessions at a moderate temperature and gradually work up to 45 minutes. Always hydrate before, during, and after your session.</p>

<h2 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1726944528295">Sauna Blanket Maintenance</h2>
    <div id="1726944528296" class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small     margin-top_none  " section-id="1726944528296">
      <p>Proper care extends your sauna blanket's lifespan significantly. Here's a practical cleaning routine based on what works for us:</p>
<br><p><strong>After every session:</strong> Wipe down the inside surface with a damp cloth or antibacterial wipe while the blanket is still slightly warm. Sweat residue is much easier to clean before it dries.</p>
<br><p><strong>Weekly:</strong> Use a gentle, non-toxic cleaning spray (Sun Home Saunas sells one specifically designed for PU leather blankets). Spray the interior, wipe with a microfiber cloth, and leave the blanket unzipped to air dry for 30 minutes.</p>
<br><p><strong>Monthly:</strong> Inspect zippers, seams, and the power cord connection for wear. Store your blanket rolled (not folded) to prevent creasing in the heating elements.</p>
