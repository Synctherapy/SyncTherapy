<section id="what-is-sauna-blanket">
<h2>What Is an Infrared Sauna Blanket?</h2>
<div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md">
<p class="mb-4">An <strong>infrared sauna blanket</strong> is a portable, foldable device that uses far infrared heat to warm your body directly — unlike a traditional sauna that heats the air around you. You lie inside the blanket, zip up, and the far infrared rays penetrate 1.5–3 inches below your skin, raising your core body temperature and triggering a deep, therapeutic sweat.</p>

<p class="mb-4">This targeted heating means you sweat faster, use less energy, and can enjoy a full sauna session in any room of your home without installing a permanent cabin. Most blankets heat from 25°C to 80°C (77°F to 176°F) and sessions typically last 30–45 minutes.</p>

<div class="bg-blue-50 p-4 rounded-lg mt-4">
<p class="text-sm text-gray-700"><strong>How it compares to a traditional sauna:</strong> Traditional saunas heat the air to 80–100°C, while infrared sauna blankets work at lower air temperatures (50–75°C) but still raise your core temperature effectively. A quality blanket costs $250–$999 compared to $3,000–$8,000+ for a cabin. For our top picks, see our <a href="/best-sauna-blanket/" class="text-primary hover:underline">best sauna blankets</a> guide.</p>

<h2>How Infrared Sauna Blankets Work</h2>
<div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md">
<p class="mb-4">The science is straightforward. Infrared sauna blankets use specialized carbon fiber heating panels that emit far infrared rays. These rays penetrate the skin and directly heat your body's tissues, rather than heating the surrounding air.</p>

<p class="mb-4">Far infrared rays are divided into wavelengths that the body absorbs efficiently, typically in the 6–14 micron range. This is why infrared blankets feel warm and comfortable at much lower ambient temperatures than traditional saunas — your body absorbs the heat directly.</p>

<section id="how-to-use">
<h2>Tips for Using a Sauna Blanket</h2>
<div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md">

<h3 class="text-xl font-semibold mb-3">Hydration</h3>
<p class="mb-4">Drink at least 16oz of water before and after your session. Keep a water bottle within reach during the session. Infrared heat penetrates deep, causing profound sweating — dehydration is the number one risk to manage.</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-semibold mb-3">Duration & Frequency</h3>
<p class="mb-4">Start with <strong>20-minute sessions at a moderate temperature</strong> (around 50°C / 122°F) to let your body adapt. Once comfortable, increase to 30–45 minutes, 2–4 times per week. Most experienced users settle between 55–70°C for their regular sessions.</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-semibold mb-3">What to Wear</h3>
<p class="mb-4">Wear lightweight, breathable cotton clothing and socks. This absorbs sweat and protects your skin from direct contact with the blanket's interior. Place a large towel inside the blanket to make cleanup easier.</p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-semibold mb-3">Safety Precautions</h3>
<ul class="space-y-2 list-disc list-inside text-gray-800 mb-4">
<li>Always follow the manufacturer's instructions</li>
<li>Stop immediately if you feel dizzy, nauseous, or excessively uncomfortable</li>
<li>Choose a <a href="/best-sauna-blanket/" class="text-primary hover:underline">low EMF sauna blanket</a> to minimize electromagnetic field exposure</li>
<li>Avoid using right before bed — wait 2–3 hours for your core temperature to drop</li>
<li>Consult your doctor if you're pregnant, have heart conditions, or take medications affecting heat tolerance</li>
</ul>
</div>
