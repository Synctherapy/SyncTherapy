            <h2 class="text-3xl font-extrabold text-gray-900 mb-6 border-b pb-4">Understanding IBS and the Microbiome</h2>
            <div class="prose max-w-none text-gray-700 text-lg leading-relaxed">
                <p>Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) affects an estimated 10-15% of the global population, yet its root causes remain complex and individualized. What we do know is that <strong>gut microbiome dysbiosis</strong> — an imbalance in the composition of intestinal bacteria — plays a significant role in triggering and worsening IBS symptoms.</p>
                <p>This is where probiotics come in. The right probiotic supplement can help:</p>
                <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2 ml-4">
                    <li>Restore microbial diversity disrupted by diet, stress, or antibiotics</li>
                    <li>Reduce intestinal inflammation that contributes to pain and discomfort</li>
                    <li>Improve gut barrier integrity, reducing "leaky gut" symptoms</li>
                    <li>Regulate bowel motility for both constipation-predominant (IBS-C) and diarrhea-predominant (IBS-D) presentations</li>
                </ul>
                <p class="mt-4">Not all probiotics are created equal for IBS. The strain-specificity matters enormously — which is exactly what we'll break down in this guide for Seed DS-01.</p>
            </div>
        </section>
