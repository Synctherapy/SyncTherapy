            <h2 class="text-3xl font-extrabold text-gray-900 mb-6 border-b pb-4">What the Research Actually Shows</h2>
            <p class="mb-6 text-lg text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">Let's be clear about something upfront: <strong>there is no such thing as a "weight loss probiotic."</strong> No probiotic supplement has been proven to directly cause weight loss. However, the relationship between gut health and weight is a legitimate area of scientific research. Here's what we know:</p>
            
            <div class="space-y-6">
                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-lg border-t-4 border-blue-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-bacteria text-blue-500 mr-2"></i>Gut Microbiome and Metabolism</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">The gut contains trillions of bacteria that influence how the body processes food, stores fat, and regulates blood sugar. Research shows that people with obesity often have less diverse gut microbiomes and different bacterial populations compared to those at a healthy weight. Certain gut bacteria can increase calorie extraction from food, potentially contributing to weight gain.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-lg border-t-4 border-purple-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-brain text-purple-500 mr-2"></i>Appetite and Hunger Signals</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Gut bacteria influence hormones that control hunger and fullness, including ghrelin (the "hunger hormone") and leptin (the "satiety hormone"). An imbalanced gut microbiome may disrupt these signals, leading to overeating or difficulty feeling satisfied. Supporting gut health may help normalize these appetite-regulating hormones.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-lg border-t-4 border-red-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-fire text-red-500 mr-2"></i>Inflammation and Weight</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Chronic low-grade inflammation is linked to obesity and metabolic syndrome. The gut microbiome plays a key role in systemic inflammation — an unhealthy gut lining can allow bacterial toxins to enter the bloodstream, triggering inflammation. Probiotics may help reduce gut inflammation, which could support metabolic health.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-lg border-t-4 border-green-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-chart-line text-green-500 mr-2"></i>Insulin Sensitivity</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Gut health affects insulin sensitivity — how efficiently the body uses insulin to process blood sugar. Poor gut health is associated with insulin resistance, which can lead to fat storage and difficulty losing weight. Some studies suggest that probiotic supplementation may improve insulin sensitivity.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>
