                <section id="how-to-choose">
                    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-6">How to Choose the Best Red Light Therapy Panel</h2>
                    <div class="space-y-6 bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                        <div>
                            <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">1. Wavelength</h4>
                            <p>This is critical. Look for <strong>660nm (Red)</strong> for skin and <strong>850nm (Near-Infrared)</strong> for deeper tissues. Advanced panels offer more wavelengths for broader effects. Understanding the <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/wavelength-for-red-light/" class="text-primary hover:underline">wavelength for red light</a> is key to choosing the right panel.</p>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                            <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">2. Power Density (Irradiance)</h4>
                            <p>Measured in mW/cm², this determines the dose of light you receive. Higher power density means shorter, more effective treatment times. Reputable brands are transparent about their irradiance measurements.</p>
                        </div>
