<section id="how-to-choose">
<h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">How to Choose the Best Red Light Therapy Panel</h2>
<div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md space-y-6">
<div class="flex items-start">
    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-primary text-xl mt-1 mr-4"></i>
    <div>
        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Wavelength</h4>
        <p>This is critical. Look for <strong>660nm (Red)</strong> for skin and <strong>850nm (Near-Infrared)</strong> for deeper tissues. Advanced panels offer more <a href="/red-light-therapy/" class="text-content-link">wavelengths</a> for broader effects.</p>
    </div>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start">
    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-primary text-xl mt-1 mr-4"></i>
    <div>
        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Power Density (Irradiance)</h4>
        <p>Measured in mW/cm², this determines the dose of light you receive. Higher power density means shorter, more effective treatment times. Reputable brands are transparent about their irradiance measurements.</p>
    </div>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start">
    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-primary text-xl mt-1 mr-4"></i>
    <div>
        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Coverage Area</h4>
        <p>Choose a panel size that matches your needs. A small, targeted panel is great for the face or specific joints, while a larger panel is better for full-body treatments.</p>
    </div>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start">
    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-primary text-xl mt-1 mr-4"></i>
    <div>
        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Safety & Certifications</h4>
        <p>Look for <a href="/fda-approved-red-light-therapy-devices-for-pain/" class="text-content-link">FDA-approved Class II medical devices</a> and other certifications (CE, UKCA). This ensures the product has been tested for safety and efficacy. Also, check for low EMF output.</p>
    </div>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start">
    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-primary text-xl mt-1 mr-4"></i>
    <div>
        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Brand Reputation & Reviews</h4>
        <p>Stick with established brands that have positive user reviews, transparent scientific data, and good customer support, including warranties and return policies.</p>
    </div>
</div>

<section id="faq">
<h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-4">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<div class="space-y-4">
<details class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm group">
    <summary class="font-semibold text-lg cursor-pointer flex justify-between items-center text-primary">
        How long does it take to see results from red light therapy?
        <i class="fas fa-chevron-down group-open:rotate-180 transition-transform"></i>
    </summary>
    <p class="mt-4 text-gray-700">Results vary depending on the condition being treated and individual factors. Some users report feeling benefits like pain relief after a few sessions, while skin improvements like reduced wrinkles may take 8-12 weeks of consistent use. Consistency is key—aim for <a href="/at-home-red-light-therapy/" class="text-content-link">3-5 sessions per week</a>.</p>
</details>

<details class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm group">
    <summary class="font-semibold text-lg cursor-pointer flex justify-between items-center text-primary">
        Is red light therapy safe to use at home?
        <i class="fas fa-chevron-down group-open:rotate-180 transition-transform"></i>
    </summary>
    <p class="mt-4 text-gray-700">Yes, red light therapy is considered safe for home use. The best red light therapy panels are FDA-cleared as Class II medical devices and have low EMF output. It's non-invasive and does not use UV light. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions for session duration and distance.</p>
</details>

<details class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm group">
    <summary class="font-semibold text-lg cursor-pointer flex justify-between items-center text-primary">
        Can I use a red light therapy panel every day?
        <i class="fas fa-chevron-down group-open:rotate-180 transition-transform"></i>
    </summary>
    <p class="mt-4 text-gray-700">Most manufacturers recommend using the panel 3-5 times per week for 10-20 minutes per session. Daily use is generally safe, but it's important to give your body time to respond to the therapy. Check the guidelines for your specific device.</p>
</details>

<details class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm group">
    <summary class="font-semibold text-lg cursor-pointer flex justify-between items-center text-primary">
        What is the difference between red light and near-infrared light?
        <i class="fas fa-chevron-down group-open:rotate-180 transition-transform"></i>
    </summary>
    <p class="mt-4 text-gray-700">Red light (typically 630-660nm) is best for skin-level issues like improving collagen, reducing wrinkles, and healing wounds. Near-infrared light (typically 810-850nm) penetrates deeper into the body, making it more effective for joint pain, muscle recovery, and inflammation.</p>
</details>
