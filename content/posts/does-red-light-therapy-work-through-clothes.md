When new clients come into my clinic to use our high-powered [clinical red light panels](/best-red-light-therapy-panels/), they almost always ask the exact same question as they step into the treatment room: 

*"Wait, do I have to take all my clothes off? Does red light therapy work through clothes?"*

As a **Certified Athletic Therapist (CAT(C))**, I understand that standing naked in front of a bright light can feel a bit strange at first! But to get the maximum clinical benefit out of your Photobiomodulation (PBM) session, we have to look at the physics of how light travels. 

Here is exactly how clothing affects your red light therapy results.

---

## The Short Answer: No, Not Really

The short and simple answer is that **red light therapy does not work well through clothes.** 

To understand why, you have to understand the fundamental goal of red light therapy: **Targeted Absorption**.

For PBM to work, the photons of light must physically penetrate your skin and reach the mitochondria inside your cells. When those photons hit a piece of fabric—whether it is a thick sweater or a thin cotton t-shirt—one of two things happens:
1.  **Absorption by Fabric:** The fabric absorbs the light energy itself (which does nothing for your body).
2.  **Reflection:** The fabric acts as a physical barrier and reflects the light away from your skin.

If the light never reaches your skin, your cells cannot absorb the energy, and you get absolutely no benefit. Your expensive [at-home red light panel](/best-red-light-therapy-panels/) essentially just becomes a very bright room heater.

## Does Light Penetrate *Thin* Clothing?

"But what if I wear a super thin, white cotton t-shirt?"

It is true that *some* photons might make it through very thin, sheer, or highly porous white fabrics. However, the energy that actually reaches your skin is drastically reduced—often by 80% or more. 

Because red light therapy works on a specific scientific dose protocol (known as irradiance), blocking 80% of the light means you are no longer getting a clinical dose. You would have to stand in front of the panel for hours to get the same benefit as a 10-minute session on bare skin.

## What Should You Wear During Treatment?

If you are treating a specific area for pain or recovery, that specific area **must** be bare. 

*   **For Knee Pain (Arthritis):** Roll your pants up or wear shorts. The knee must be exposed.
*   **For Lower Back Pain:** Lift your shirt up so the panel is shining directly onto the bare skin of your lumbar spine.
*   **For Facial Skin/Acne:** Ensure you have no makeup on. Makeup (especially foundation with SPF) acts exactly like clothing and blocks the light!

If you are doing a full-body wellness treatment for systemic inflammation or hormonal balance, the most effective way to use your panel is in your underwear or completely nude. 

### Does It Penetrate Through Hair?

The same physical rules apply to hair. If you have a very thick, dense beard or long, dark hair, the light will not easily reach the skin underneath. If you are using [red light therapy for hair growth](/red-light-therapy-for-hair-growth/), it is critical to part your hair or use a device that sits directly against the scalp so the light can actually reach the hair follicle roots.
