        <section id="what-are-stretch-marks">
            <h2>Understanding Stretch Marks: What Causes Striae Distensae?</h2>
            <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">Stretch marks, medically termed striae distensae, develop when skin stretches beyond its elastic capacity, causing the dermal collagen and elastin fibers to tear. This results in linear scars that initially appear red or purple (striae rubra) before fading to silvery-white (striae alba).</p>
            
            <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                <h3 class="!mt-0">Common Causes of Stretch Marks</h3>
                <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2"><i class="fas fa-baby text-primary mr-2"></i>Pregnancy</h4>
                        <p class="text-gray-600">Affects 50-90% of expectant mothers as skin rapidly expands</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2"><i class="fas fa-chart-line text-primary mr-2"></i>Rapid Weight Changes</h4>
                        <p class="text-gray-600">Quick weight gain or muscle growth stretches skin faster than collagen can adapt</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2"><i class="fas fa-child text-primary mr-2"></i>Growth Spurts</h4>
                        <p class="text-gray-600">Adolescent growth during puberty can outpace skin elasticity</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2"><i class="fas fa-dna text-primary mr-2"></i>Hormonal & Genetic Factors</h4>
                        <p class="text-gray-600">Corticosteroid use and genetic predisposition affect collagen structure</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
            </div>

            <p class="mt-6 text-gray-700">The formation process involves mechanical stretching combined with hormonal influences that alter collagen structure. When skin stretches too quickly, the middle layer (dermis) literally tears, creating the characteristic linear marks that can significantly impact confidence and quality of life.</p>
        </section>
