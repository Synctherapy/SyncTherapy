## Understanding Stretch Marks: What Causes Striae Distensae?
Stretch marks, medically termed striae distensae, develop when skin stretches beyond its elastic capacity, causing the dermal collagen and elastin fibers to tear. This results in linear scars that initially appear red or purple (striae rubra) before fading to silvery-white (striae alba).
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_2\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
The formation process involves mechanical stretching combined with hormonal influences that alter collagen structure. When skin stretches too quickly, the middle layer (dermis) literally tears, creating the characteristic linear marks that can significantly impact confidence and quality of life.
## The Science: How Does Red Light Therapy Work for Stretch Marks?
Red light therapy, also known as photobiomodulation or low-level light therapy, uses specific wavelengths of red and near-infrared light (630-850nm) to trigger biological responses in skin tissue. Unlike UV light, which damages skin, therapeutic red light penetrates safely to initiate healing cascades. Research has shown that [red light therapy benefits](/red-light-therapy/) include reducing inflammation, promoting collagen production, and accelerating tissue repair. Many individuals report improvements in skin tone and texture, making it a popular choice for skincare and recovery. Additionally, athletes often utilize this therapy to enhance muscle recovery and alleviate soreness.

Devices offering both wavelengths produce the most comprehensive results.

The convenience and affordability make red light therapy more sustainable for the extended treatment period needed.
