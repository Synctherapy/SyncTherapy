## \\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_26\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_ Frequently Asked Questions
Does red light therapy help fibromyalgia?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_27\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Yes, clinical research supports red light therapy for fibromyalgia. A 2019 meta-analysis of 9 randomized controlled trials found that approximately 95% of fibromyalgia patients receiving low-level laser therapy showed statistically significant improvements in pain, fatigue, depression, and anxiety. A 2025 umbrella review found "moderate certainty evidence" supporting photobiomodulation for fibromyalgia fatigue specifically.
How often should I use red light therapy for fibromyalgia?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_28\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Clinical studies typically use 2-3 sessions per week for 4-12 weeks initially. Each session lasts 15-20 minutes. Start conservatively and gradually increase based on your response. Consistency is more important than frequency.
What is the best red light therapy device for fibromyalgia?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_29\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
For fibromyalgia, larger panels that can treat multiple body areas simultaneously work best. Top recommendations include PlatinumLED BioMax for highest power, Mito Red Light MitoPRO for best value, and Kala Red Light or Rouge for Canadians.
Is red light therapy safe for fibromyalgia patients?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_30\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Yes, red light therapy is considered very safe with minimal side effects. Clinical trials consistently report no serious adverse events. Unlike medications commonly prescribed for fibromyalgia, red light therapy doesn't cause dependency or systemic side effects.
Can red light therapy make fibromyalgia worse?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_31\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Red light therapy is unlikely to worsen fibromyalgia when used correctly. However, too much light exposure can reduce effectiveness. Start with shorter sessions (5-10 minutes) and gradually increase. If you experience increased symptoms, reduce treatment time.
How long does it take to see results?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_32\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Most studies show meaningful improvements after 4-6 weeks of consistent treatment. Some patients report feeling benefits within the first few sessions, while others need 8-12 weeks. A 2023 study found some benefits remained detectable even at 24 weeks post-treatment.
Does red light therapy hurt?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_33\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
No, red light therapy is painless. You may feel mild warmth from the light, but it's generally a pleasant, relaxing experience. This is particularly important for fibromyalgia patients who are often sensitive to stimulation.
What wavelengths are best for fibromyalgia?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_34\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
A combination of red light (630-660nm) and near-infrared light (810-850nm) works best. Red light penetrates superficial tissues while near-infrared penetrates deeper to reach muscles and joints. Learn more about optimal [wavelengths for red light therapy](/red-light-therapy/).
