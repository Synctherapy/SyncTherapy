            <h2 class="text-3xl font-extrabold text-gray-900 border-b pb-4">Key Differences Explained</h2>

            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-1 gap-8">
                <!-- Daily Wellness vs Clinical-Grade -->
                <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-2xl shadow-lg border-l-4 border-accent">
                    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4"><i class="fas fa-heartbeat text-accent mr-2"></i>Daily Wellness vs Clinical-Grade</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed mb-4"><strong>Seed DS-01</strong> is designed as a daily synbiotic for proactive wellness. It takes an "ecosystem support" approach, combining 24 strains with prebiotics to support not just gut health but also skin clarity, heart health, and immune function. It's ideal for otherwise healthy individuals looking to optimize their microbiome and prevent digestive issues.</p>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed"><strong>Visbiome</strong>, by contrast, is a medical-grade probiotic designed specifically for therapeutic use. With 112.5 billion CFU and decades of research behind it, it's been clinically shown to help manage ulcerative colitis flare-ups, maintain remission in pouchitis patients, and reduce IBS symptoms. This is not a daily wellness supplement — it's a targeted intervention for specific GI conditions.</p>
                </div>

                <!-- Shelf-Stable vs Refrigerated -->
                <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-2xl shadow-lg border-l-4 border-primary">
                    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4"><i class="fas fa-snowflake text-primary mr-2"></i>Shelf-Stable vs Refrigerated</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed mb-4">One of the biggest practical differences is <strong>storage</strong>. Seed DS-01 uses patented <strong>ViaCap®</strong> technology — a capsule-within-a-capsule that protects the probiotics from stomach acid, heat, moisture, and oxygen. This makes it completely shelf-stable. You can leave it on your counter, travel with it, and never worry about refrigeration.</p>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">Visbiome, however, <strong>requires refrigeration</strong> to maintain potency. The high-CFU formula contains sensitive strains that degrade quickly at room temperature. This means:</p>
                    <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2 ml-4 mt-4 text-gray-700">
                        <li>You must store it in your fridge at all times</li>
                        <li>Traveling with Visbiome is logistically challenging</li>
                        <li>Missing even a day or two of refrigeration can reduce effectiveness</li>
                    </ul>
                    <p class="mt-4 text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">For convenience alone, Seed wins this category hands down.</p>
                </div>

                <!-- Strain Diversity vs Targeted Potency -->
                <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-2xl shadow-lg border-l-4 border-accent">
                    <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4"><i class="fas fa-leaf text-accent mr-2"></i>Strain Diversity vs Targeted Potency</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed mb-4"><strong>Seed offers 24 strains</strong> — a high-diversity approach designed to support the entire gut ecosystem. The logic: the more strains you have, the more bases you cover. Seed's strains are organized into four blends targeting digestive health, skin health, heart health, and micronutrient synthesis.</p>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed"><strong>Visbiome offers 8 strains</strong> but at a much higher CFU (112.5 billion). This is a "less is more" approach with a therapeutic dose. The specific strains in Visbiome have been selected based on their clinical performance in IBD and IBS trials — it's not about diversity, it's about having the right strains at the right dose.</p>
                </div>
