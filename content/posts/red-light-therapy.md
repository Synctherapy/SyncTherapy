            <h2><i class="fas fa-lightbulb text-accent mr-2"></i>What Is Red Light Therapy?</h2>
            <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                <p class="mb-4"><strong>Red light therapy (RLT)</strong>, also known as <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/photobiomodulation-explained/" class="text-primary hover:underline">photobiomodulation</a> (PBM) or low-level light therapy (LLLT), is a non-invasive treatment that uses specific wavelengths of red and near-infrared light to stimulate cellular function.</p>
                
                <p class="mb-4">The therapy delivers light energy at <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/wavelength-for-red-light/" class="text-primary hover:underline">wavelengths between 630-850 nanometers</a>, which can penetrate skin tissue to varying depths:</p>

                <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-6">
                    <div class="bg-red-50 p-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-red-500">
                        <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-2" style="color: #dc2626;"><i class="fas fa-circle mr-2" style="color: #dc2626;"></i>Red Light (630-700nm)</h3>
                        <p class="text-gray-700 text-sm"><strong>Penetration:</strong> 8-10mm into tissue</p>
                        <p class="text-gray-700 text-sm"><strong>Best for:</strong> Skin conditions, surface-level concerns, collagen production</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="bg-purple-50 p-4 rounded-lg border-l-4 border-purple-500">
                        <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-2" style="color: #7c3aed;"><i class="fas fa-circle mr-2" style="color: #7c3aed;"></i>Near-Infrared (700-850nm)</h3>
                        <p class="text-gray-700 text-sm"><strong>Penetration:</strong> 30-40mm deep</p>
                        <p class="text-gray-700 text-sm"><strong>Best for:</strong> Muscles, joints, bone tissue, deep healing</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-gray-100 p-4 rounded-lg mb-6">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-4">A Brief History of Red Light Therapy</h3>
                    <div class="space-y-3 text-gray-700">
                        <p><strong>1960s:</strong> Hungarian scientist Endre Mester discovered therapeutic potential accidentally while studying whether laser light might cause cancer in mice. Instead, the mice grew hair faster and healed wounds more quickly.</p>
                        <p><strong>1990s:</strong> NASA advanced this research using LED technology to promote wound healing and plant growth in space, demonstrating that red and near-infrared light could accelerate healing in astronauts.</p>
                        <p><strong>Early 2000s:</strong> First FDA clearances for light therapy devices were granted.</p>
                        <p><strong>Today:</strong> Photobiomodulation is used in clinical settings worldwide, from dermatology offices to sports medicine facilities.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
