            <section id="how-it-works">
                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">How Reverse Osmosis Filtration Works</h2>
                <div class="space-y-6 bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                    <img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/WjteqtsSlmT5vf15hDjH.jpg" onerror="this.onerror=null;this.src='https://placehold.co/350x350/CCCCCC/333333?text=RO+Countertop';" alt="A countertop reverse osmosis filter for home use." class="rounded-lg shadow-md float-right ml-6 mb-4 w-full sm:w-1/3">
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">The Core Technology: The RO Membrane</h4>
                        <p>The quest for exceptional drinking water purity leads many to a reverse osmosis (RO) system. This advanced water purification technology works by forcing water through a semipermeable <strong>membrane</strong>‚Äîa critical component with microscopic pores. This membrane acts as an ultra-fine filter, blocking virtually all dissolved solids, heavy metals (like lead and arsenic), chemicals (like fluoride and chlorine), and other impurities from passing through. Wondering how it stacks up against other methods? See our reverse osmosis vs. distilled water comparison.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Multi-Stage Filtration for Max Purity</h4>
                        <p>A complete RO system doesn't just rely on the membrane. It uses a multi-stage process. <strong>Pre-filters</strong> first remove larger particles like sediment, silt, and rust, which protects the delicate RO membrane. After passing through the membrane, the water often goes through a <strong>post-filter</strong> (typically a carbon filter) to polish the taste and remove any lingering odors or tastes, ensuring the final product is both pure and refreshing.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">The Result: Clean, Safe, and Great-Tasting Water</h4>
                        <p>The result of this comprehensive reverse osmosis filtration is a significant improvement in water quality. RO systems can remove up to 99% of total dissolved solids (TDS), providing water that is not only safer but also tastes noticeably cleaner and crisper. For those with concerns about specific contaminants in their tap water, a reverse osmosis water filter system offers one of the most effective and reliable solutions available for home use. But is reverse osmosis water good for you in the long run? We explore the topic in detail.</p>
