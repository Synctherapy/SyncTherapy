## Frequently Asked Questions
How much do commercial red light therapy beds cost?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_18\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Commercial beds range from $21,000 (Rouge Horizon G4) to $120,000+ (NovoTHOR). Mid-range options like MitoRECHARGE ($28k) and TheraLight 360 ($60-80k) offer different trade-offs between features and budget. DIY panel setups can achieve similar results for $3,000-5,000.
Are red light therapy beds better than panels?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_19\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Beds provide simultaneous full-body coverage (front and back), reducing session time from 20-40 minutes with panels to 10-15 minutes. This is critical for commercial operations where throughput matters. For home use, panels are more cost-effective and can achieve similar results with longer or multiple sessions.
What electrical requirements do I need?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_20\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
It varies by model. MitoRECHARGE and Prism Light Pod run on standard 110V outlets. TheraLight 360 and NovoTHOR require dedicated 220V/30A circuits (electrician install). Rouge Horizon G4 is universal voltage (100-240V). Budget $500-2,000 for electrical upgrades if needed.
What's the ROI for adding a bed to my clinic?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_21\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
At $60/session with 6 sessions/day, 5 days/week, you'd generate ~$78,000/year. A Rouge Horizon G4 ($21k) pays back in 3-4 months. Premium beds like TheraLight ($70k) take 10-12 months. Most clinics also report increased patient retention and referrals from the "wow factor." Use our [ROI Calculator](#roi-calculator) above to run your own numbers.
Are red light therapy beds safe?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_22\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Yes, when used as directed. Unlike UV tanning beds, red/NIR light doesn't cause DNA damage or increase skin cancer risk. All beds listed are FDA-registered or cleared. Contraindications include photosensitivity disorders, hyperthyroidism, pregnancy (caution), and active cancer. Eye protection is required.
