        <section id="key-differences" class="scroll-mt-24 space-y-8">
            <h2 class="text-3xl font-extrabold text-gray-900 border-b pb-4">Key Differences That Matter</h2>

            <div class="prose max-w-none text-gray-700 text-lg leading-relaxed">
                <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mt-8 mb-4">1. Strain Diversity and Quality</h3>
                <p>Seed DS-01 contains 24 clinically studied probiotic strains, each selected for specific health benefits. The strains include well-researched options like <em>Lactiplantibacillus plantarum</em> for cardiovascular health, <em>Bifidobacterium longum</em> for gut barrier function, and <em>Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus</em> for skin health.</p>
                <p>Physician's Choice includes 10 strains with research backing, but the strain count is less than half of Seed's. For someone seeking comprehensive microbiome support across multiple body systems, Seed's broader diversity is advantageous.</p>

                <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mt-8 mb-4">2. Delivery Technology</h3>
                <p>Seed's ViaCap® technology is a capsule-in-capsule system designed to ensure probiotics survive stomach acid and reach the intestines alive. This is a significant differentiator — many probiotics don't survive the harsh gastric environment.</p>
                <p>Physician's Choice uses standard capsules without any specialized delivery system. While some strains may survive naturally, the lack of protective technology means potentially fewer viable bacteria reach the gut.</p>

                <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mt-8 mb-4">3. Clinical Evidence</h3>
                <p>Seed has published multiple product-specific clinical trials in 2024-2026, including:</p>
                <ul class="space-y-2 mt-4">
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-3"></i><strong>Allegretti et al. (2026):</strong> DBRCT with n=350 participants showed significant reduction in bloating and gas vs placebo</li>
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-3"></i><strong>Napier et al. (2025):</strong> 91-day trial demonstrated 100-fold increase in Urolithin A and increased butyrate production</li>
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-3"></i><strong>DDW 2024:</strong> 12-week IBS trial showed reduced abdominal pain and improved stool consistency</li>
                </ul>
                <p class="mt-4">Physician's Choice does not publish its own clinical trials. The product relies on strain-level research from academic studies, but there's no finished-product evidence showing the specific combination works as marketed.</p>

                <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mt-8 mb-4">4. Prebiotic Formulation</h3>
                <p>Both products include prebiotics, but they differ significantly:</p>
                <ul class="space-y-2 mt-4">
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-3"></i><strong>Seed:</strong> Pomegranate punicalagins + pine bark polyphenols — non-fermenting prebiotics that won't cause bloating</li>
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-3"></i><strong>Physician's Choice:</strong> Organic prebiotic fiber blend — likely includes inulin or FOS, which can cause gas and bloating in sensitive individuals</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
