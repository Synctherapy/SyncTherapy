                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">How Hydrogen Water Works: A Deeper Dive</h2>
                <p class="mb-4">The therapeutic potential of hydrogen water comes from the unique properties of molecular hydrogen (H₂). As the smallest molecule in the universe, it can easily diffuse across cell membranes to exert its effects. The primary mechanisms are:</p>

                    <div>
                        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Selective Antioxidant Action</h4>
                        <p>The most cited benefit is H₂'s role as a selective antioxidant. It specifically neutralizes the most harmful free radicals, such as the hydroxyl radical (·OH) and peroxynitrite (ONOO−), which cause significant cellular damage. Unlike other antioxidants, it doesn't interfere with beneficial reactive oxygen species (ROS) that are essential for normal cell signaling. This targeted approach helps reduce pathological oxidative stress without disrupting cellular balance, as detailed in a <a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10816294/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">systematic review</a>.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Anti-Inflammatory Effects</h4>
                        <p>Molecular hydrogen has been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory effects. It can down-regulate pro-inflammatory pathways like NF-κB, which in turn suppresses the release of inflammatory cytokines. A <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32699287/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">randomized controlled trial</a> on healthy adults found that hydrogen-rich water reduced inflammatory responses and prevented the apoptosis (cell death) of peripheral blood cells.</p>
                    </div>
                     <div>
                        <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-primary">Mitochondrial Protection</h4>
                        <p>By protecting mitochondria—the energy powerhouses of our cells—from oxidative damage, H₂ may enhance their efficiency in producing ATP (energy). This bioenergetic mechanism provides a plausible explanation for the anti-fatigue effects reported in some clinical studies, making it highly relevant for athletes.</p>
