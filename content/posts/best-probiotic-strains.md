## Frequently Asked Questions
What is the difference between probiotics and digestive enzymes?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_19\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
\\\*\\\*Probiotics\\\*\\\* are live beneficial bacteria that colonize your gut to improve its overall health and balance. \\\*\\\*Digestive enzymes\\\*\\\* are non-living proteins that help break down specific foods (like fats, carbs, and proteins) in your digestive tract. While both aid digestion, probiotics work on the ecosystem, while enzymes work on the food itself. Learn more in our probiotics vs. digestive enzymes guide.
Can probiotics help you lose weight?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_20\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Some research suggests a link between gut health and weight management. Certain probiotic strains may influence metabolism, inflammation, and appetite hormones. However, probiotics are not a magic weight-loss pill. They are best used to support overall gut health as part of a balanced diet and healthy lifestyle. See our guide to learn [how probiotics can help you lose weight](/best-prebiotics-and-probiotics-supplements/).
How long should I take a probiotic?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_21\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
For most people, probiotics are considered a long-term supplement for maintaining gut health. The beneficial bacteria only reside in your gut temporarily as they pass through, so consistent daily intake is required to maintain their benefits. It's best to think of it as part of your daily health routine, much like taking a multivitamin. To know when to take them for best results, see our guide on the [best time to take probiotics](/best-prebiotics-and-probiotics-supplements/).
