                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">What Are Soil-Based Organisms Probiotics?</h2>
                <div class="space-y-6 bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md">
                    <p>Soil-based organisms (SBOs) are beneficial bacteria that originate in the soil. Unlike traditional probiotics derived from dairy (like <em>Lactobacillus</em> and <em>Bifidobacterium</em>), soil-based probiotics are spore-forming. This means they are protected by a hard, natural shell that allows them to survive the harsh, acidic environment of the stomach.</p>
                    <p>This superior survivability is their key advantage. While many traditional probiotics die off before reaching your intestines, SBOs arrive intact, ready to colonize and confer their health benefits where they matter most. They more closely resemble the microbes our ancestors consumed through foraged, farm-fresh foods, making them uniquely suited to the human gut.</p>
