faqs:
  - question: Why is my Echo Flask not producing hydrogen?
    answer: "The most common causes are mineral buildup on the electrodes, low battery, or using unfiltered water. Run a monthly vinegar descaling cycle, charge the Flask fully, and use filtered water. If the issue persists after cleaning, contact Echo Water support."
  - question: Why is my Echo Flask not charging?
    answer: "Check the USB-C cable and power source. Try a different cable and adapter. Inspect the charging port for debris or moisture. If the port is wet, let it dry completely before charging. If none of these work, the issue may be covered under warranty."
  - question: What does the red light mean on the Echo Flask?
    answer: "A red light on the Echo Flask typically indicates a low battery or a device error. Charge the Flask fully and try again. If the red light persists after charging, contact Echo Water support as it may indicate a hardware issue covered under warranty."
  - question: Why won't my Echo Flask connect to the app?
    answer: "Make sure Bluetooth is enabled, the Flask is powered on and nearby, and the app is updated to the latest version. Try removing the device from your Bluetooth list and re-pairing. Restart both the phone and the Flask if needed."

            <ol class="space-y-3 text-gray-700">
                <li class="flex items-start"><span class="bg-primary text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center mr-3 flex-shrink-0 text-sm">1</span><strong>Check battery level:</strong> A low battery can prevent the electrolysis process from running properly. Charge fully and try again.</li>
                <li class="flex items-start"><span class="bg-primary text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center mr-3 flex-shrink-0 text-sm">2</span><strong>Use filtered water:</strong> Tap water with high mineral content or contaminants can interfere with hydrogen generation. Always use filtered water.</li>
                <li class="flex items-start"><span class="bg-primary text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center mr-3 flex-shrink-0 text-sm">3</span><strong>Descale the electrodes:</strong> Mineral buildup is the most common cause. Run a <a href="/echo-flask-cleaning/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">monthly vinegar descaling cycle</a> using 1/3 white vinegar and 2/3 warm distilled water, followed by the Clean Flask cycle.</li>
                <li class="flex items-start"><span class="bg-primary text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center mr-3 flex-shrink-0 text-sm">4</span><strong>Check the gasket:</strong> Make sure the cap gasket is properly seated with the line side facing inward. A loose gasket can affect the seal.</li>
            </ol>
            <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mt-4">If the Flask still doesn't produce hydrogen after cleaning and charging, contact Echo Water support. This may be a hardware issue covered under the <a href="/echo-flask-warranty/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">5-year warranty</a>.</p>

                <li class="flex items-start"><span class="bg-primary text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center mr-3 flex-shrink-0 text-sm">2</span><strong>Try a different power source:</strong> Switch from a laptop USB port to a wall adapter, or vice versa.</li>
                <li class="flex items-start"><span class="bg-primary text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center mr-3 flex-shrink-0 text-sm">3</span><strong>Check the charging port:</strong> Look for debris, lint, or moisture in the USB-C port.
