## Understanding Hashimoto's: The Autoimmune Attack on Your Thyroid
Hashimoto's thyroiditis is the most common cause of hypothyroidism in countries like Canada and the US. According to the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/hashimotos-disease/symptoms-causes/syc-20351855), it's an autoimmune disorder where your immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid gland, causing chronic [inflammation](/red-light-therapy/) and gradually destroying healthy tissue.
This attack is driven by two main antibodies:
\\\* \\\*\\\*Thyroid Peroxidase Antibodies (TPOAb)\\\*\\\*
\\\* \\\*\\\*Thyroglobulin Antibodies (TgAb)\\\*\\\*
Conventional treatment involves lifelong levothyroxine (LT4) hormone replacement. However, this only manages the hormone deficiency; it doesn't stop the underlying autoimmune attack that causes the persistent symptoms.

## Red Light Therapy for Thyroid Nodules
A common concern is whether red light therapy is appropriate for those with thyroid nodules. The research in this specific area is still emerging. However, the foundational mechanism of PBM is to help restore normal cellular function rather than causing abnormal growth. A systematic review on the oncological safety of PBM found that it tends to enhance the health of normal cells while having no effect or even an inhibitory effect on neoplastic (abnormal) cells. To date, clinical trials have not linked PBM to the development of malignant nodules. Despite this promising safety profile, it is absolutely essential to consult with your endocrinologist before using red light therapy if you have thyroid nodules.
