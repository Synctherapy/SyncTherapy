faqs:
  - question: How many Seed probiotics should I take daily?
    answer: "The standard recommended dose is 2 capsules per day. Seed suggests taking 2 capsules daily with food, preferably at the same time each day for consistent results. Each serving delivers 53.6 billion AFU across 24 probiotic strains with 400mg of prebiotics (pomegranate punicalagins and pine bark polyphenols)."
  - question: Can I take more than 2 capsules of Seed probiotics?
    answer: "Yes, Seed recommends a loading phase of up to 4 capsules daily for the first 2-3 weeks when beginning supplementation or after antibiotic use. This higher dose helps establish bacterial colonization more rapidly. After the loading phase, you can maintain with the standard 2 capsules daily."
  - question: Why isn't more always better with probiotics?
    answer: "More isn't always better because probiotic strains compete for resources and space in the gut. Taking excessive doses can actually reduce effectiveness as strains may compete rather than complement each other. Additionally, high doses may increase side effects like bloating initially. The 2-capsule daily maintenance dose was formulated based on clinical trials to deliver optimal results without overwhelming the gut ecosystem."
  - question: How long should I take the loading dose?
    answer: "The loading phase typically lasts 2-3 weeks (14-21 days). This gives the probiotic strains time to establish colonization in the gut. After the loading phase, most users transition to the standard 2-capsule daily maintenance dose. Those with significant gut issues may benefit from longer loading phases under practitioner guidance."
  - question: Can I take Seed probiotics twice a day?
    answer: "Yes, you can split the dose by taking 1 capsule twice daily (morning and evening) instead of 2 capsules at once. This may help improve tolerance and consistent delivery throughout the day. Taking with meals is generally recommended to help protect the probiotics from stomach acid."

        <section id="why-more-isnt-better" class="scroll-mt-24">
            <h2 class="text-3xl font-extrabold text-gray-900 mb-6 border-b pb-4">Why More Isn't Always Better: The Clinical Perspective</h2>
            <p class="mb-6 text-lg text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">It might seem logical that if 2 capsules are good, 4 or 6 must be better. But probiotic dosing doesn't work that way. Here's the clinical reality:</p>
            
            <div class="space-y-6">
                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md border-l-4 border-blue-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-users text-blue-500 mr-2"></i>1. Strains Compete for Resources</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Your gut has limited space and resources. When you overwhelm it with excessive probiotic strains, they compete rather than complement each other. The 2-capsule maintenance dose was formulated based on clinical trials to deliver optimal strain ratios without overwhelming the ecosystem.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md border-l-4 border-amber-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-bomb text-amber-500 mr-2"></i>2. Die-Off Reactions</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Higher doses can trigger more pronounced Herxheimer (die-off) reactions as harmful bacteria are displaced. Symptoms include bloating, gas, and temporary digestive discomfort. Starting with 2 capsules and building up (or using the loading phase) minimizes these effects.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md border-l-4 border-green-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-flask text-green-500 mr-2"></i>3. Clinical Trials Set the Standard</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Seed's clinical trials used specific dosing protocols. The 53.6 billion AFU in 2 capsules was proven effective in their 2024-2026 research trials. Taking more hasn't been shown to deliver proportionally better results — it's simply not studied because the maintenance dose already works.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-md border-l-4 border-purple-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-money-bill-wave text-purple-500 mr-2"></i>4. Cost vs. Benefit</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Doubling your dose doubles your cost without doubling (or even proportionally increasing) benefits. The maintenance dose is designed for long-term sustainability — both for your gut and your wallet.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
