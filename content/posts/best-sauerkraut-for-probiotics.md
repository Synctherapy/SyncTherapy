            <section id="science-of-kraut">
                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">The Science: How Sauerkraut Benefits Your Gut</h2>
                <p class="mb-6 text-gray-700">Sauerkraut is a synbiotic powerhouse. This means it delivers both <strong>probiotics</strong> (live beneficial bacteria) and <strong>prebiotics</strong> (the fiber from cabbage that feeds those bacteria), making it a uniquely effective food for gut health.</p>
                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-md border-t-4 border-accent space-y-4">
                    <div>
                        <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold">A Symphony of Live Microbes</h3>
                        <p>Through natural lacto-fermentation, cabbage develops a rich diversity of Lactic Acid Bacteria, primarily from the highly beneficial <strong>Lactobacillus</strong> genus. A single serving can contain up to 28 different strains. These microbes help balance your gut flora, support digestion, and strengthen your immune system.</p>
                    </div>
                    <blockquote class="border-l-4 border-primary bg-blue-50 p-4 my-6 rounded-r-lg">
                        <h4 class="font-bold text-primary mb-2"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb mr-2"></i>Did You Know?</h4>
                        <p class="text-gray-700 italic">The fermentation process makes the nutrients in cabbage, like Vitamin C and K, more bioavailable. The bacteria essentially "pre-digest" the food, making it easier for your body to absorb its benefits.</p>
                    </blockquote>
                </div>
            </section>
