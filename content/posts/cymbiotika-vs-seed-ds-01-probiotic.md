## Frequently Asked Questions
What is a 'synbiotic' and why does it matter?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_10\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
A synbiotic is a supplement that contains both probiotics (good bacteria) and prebiotics (food for the good bacteria). This combination is beneficial because the prebiotics help the probiotics to survive, thrive, and colonize in your gut, making the supplement more effective. Seed DS-01 is a synbiotic.
Why is the delivery capsule so important for probiotics?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_11\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Your stomach is highly acidic and can destroy many probiotic bacteria before they ever reach your intestines. An advanced delivery system, like Seed's ViaCap®, protects the live organisms from stomach acid, ensuring they are released in the colon where they can provide the most benefit. This dramatically increases the supplement's effectiveness.
What are AFUs and how do they compare to CFUs?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_12\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
CFU (Colony-Forming Units) is an older method of estimating the number of viable bacteria. AFU (Active Fluorescent Units) is a more precise, modern method using flow cytometry to count viable cells directly. It's considered a more accurate measure of a probiotic's potency, and it's the standard Seed uses.
Can I take Seed or Cymbiotika with other supplements?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_13\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Generally, yes. Both Seed and Cymbiotika are designed to be safe to take alongside most other vitamins and supplements. However, it's always best to consult with your healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially if you are taking prescription medications, to ensure there are no potential interactions.
