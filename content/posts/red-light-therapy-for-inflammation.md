<h2 style="--fontsize: 54; line-height: 1.14;" data-lineheight="61.56px" data-fontsize="54" class="fusion-responsive-typography-calculated">Adverse Effects and Contraindications</h2>

<p>Red light therapy is generally considered safe, but like any treatment, it has potential <strong>adverse effects</strong> and there are certain <strong>contraindications</strong> to keep in mind.</p>
<p><strong>Adverse Effects:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mild and Temporary Side Effects:</strong></li>
<li>Individuals may experience transient side effects such as headaches, eye strain, or irritability following exposure.</li>
<li><strong>Overuse:</strong></li>
<li>Overexposure can lead to skin redness and irritation.</li>
</ul>
<p>Careful adherence to guidelines can minimize these effects.</p>
<p><strong>Contraindications:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Photosensitivity:</strong></li>
<li>Individuals with skin conditions that make them sensitive to light, such as lupus or porphyria, should avoid this therapy.</li>
<li><strong>Medications:</strong></li>
<li>Those on photosensitizing medications should also exercise caution.</li>
<li><strong>Eye Safety:</strong></li>
<li>Though not common, there is a risk to eye health if proper protection is not used during treatment.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Preexisting Conditions:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Cancer:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Patients with active cancer, particularly skin cancer, should consult a healthcare provider due to concerns about the proliferative effects of light therapy on cells.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<strong>Pregnancy:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Pregnant women are often advised to avoid red light therapy as the effects on the fetus are not well-studied. Read more about red light therapy and pregnancy.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
