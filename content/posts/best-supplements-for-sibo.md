## Frequently Asked Questions
Why are spore-based probiotics better for SIBO?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_16\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Traditional probiotics (like Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium) can sometimes add more bacteria to an already overcrowded small intestine, potentially making symptoms worse. Spore-based probiotics have a protective shell that allows them to survive stomach acid. They don't colonize the small intestine but rather act as "gut police," helping to eliminate bad bacteria and encourage your own good bacteria to grow, making them much safer and more effective for SIBO.
What is the best natural prokinetic for SIBO?

Ginger is one of the most effective and well-researched natural prokinetics. It helps stimulate the migrating motor complex (MMC), the "cleansing waves" that sweep debris and bacteria out of the small intestine between meals. The ginger extract in supplements like \\\*\\\*Candibactin-BR\\\*\\\* can provide this prokinetic support as part of a treatment protocol.
Can I take these supplements with prescription antibiotics like Rifaximin?

This should always be discussed with your healthcare provider. However, many practitioners recommend using herbal antimicrobials either as a standalone treatment or in rotation after a course of antibiotics. Some studies suggest that certain supplements, like partially hydrolyzed guar gum (PHGG), can even enhance the effectiveness of Rifaximin. Do not combine treatments without professional guidance.
