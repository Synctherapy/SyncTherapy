            <section id="what-is-sibo">
                <h2>Understanding SIBO: Symptoms & Diagnosis</h2>
                <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md space-y-6">
                    <div>
                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold">What is SIBO?</h3>
                        <p class="mt-2 text-gray-700">SIBO, or Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth, is a condition where there's an abnormal increase in the number of bacteria in the small intestine.<sup><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28716079" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-primary">[2]</a></sup> Normally, the small intestine has relatively few bacteria. When they overgrow, they can ferment carbohydrates from food too early, leading to gas, bloating, and other painful symptoms. This overgrowth can also interfere with normal digestion and nutrient absorption.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold">Common Symptoms of SIBO</h3>
                        <p class="mt-2 text-gray-700">SIBO symptoms can vary widely but often include:</p>
                        <ul class="grid grid-cols-1 sm:grid-cols-2 gap-x-8 gap-y-2 mt-4 list-disc list-inside">
                            <li>Abdominal bloating and distension</li>
                            <li>Excessive gas and flatulence</li>
                            <li>Abdominal pain or cramping</li>
                            <li>Diarrhea, constipation, or both</li>
                            <li>An uncomfortable feeling of fullness</li>
                            <li><a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/best-probiotic-for-acid-reflux/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">Acid reflux</a> or heartburn</li>
                            <li>Nausea</li>
                            <li>Fatigue and brain fog<sup><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29915215" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-primary">[3]</a></sup></li>
                        </ul>
                    </div>
                     <div>
                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold">How is SIBO Diagnosed?</h3>
                        <p class="mt-2 text-gray-700">The most common and non-invasive way to diagnose SIBO is with a <strong>breath test</strong>. After drinking a sugar solution (lactulose or glucose), your breath is collected at intervals to measure the levels of hydrogen and methane gas produced by intestinal bacteria. Elevated levels of these gases at an early time point indicate an overgrowth in the small intestine.</p>
                        <ul class="mt-4 list-disc list-inside space-y-1">
                            <li><strong>Hydrogen-Dominant SIBO:</strong> Often associated with diarrhea.</li>
                            <li><strong>Methane-Dominant SIBO (IMO):</strong> Often associated with constipation.</li>

                            Why are spore-based probiotics better for SIBO?
                            <i class="fas fa-chevron-down group-open:rotate-180 transition-transform"></i>
                        </summary>
                        <p class="mt-4 text-gray-700">Traditional probiotics (like Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium) can sometimes add more bacteria to an already overcrowded small intestine, potentially making symptoms worse. Spore-based probiotics have a protective shell that allows them to survive stomach acid. They don't colonize the small intestine but rather act as "gut police," helping to eliminate bad bacteria and encourage your own good bacteria to grow, making them much safer and more effective for SIBO.</p>

It helps stimulate the migrating motor complex (MMC), the "cleansing waves" that sweep debris and bacteria out of the small intestine between meals. The ginger extract in supplements like <strong>Candibactin-BR</strong> can provide this prokinetic support as part of a treatment protocol.</p>
