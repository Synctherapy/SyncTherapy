<h3>Dissecting Androgenetic Alopecia: The Common Culprit</h3>
<div>
<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8906269/" target="_blank">Androgenetic alopecia</a> is the most prevalent form of hair loss, characterized by a receding hairline and thinning hair across the scalp. It results from a combination of genetic and hormonal factors, leading to a progressive reduction in hair density. Understanding its mechanisms is crucial for identifying effective treatments</div>
<div></div>
<h3>Unraveling Alopecia Areata: An Autoimmune Response</h3>
<div></div>
<div>Alopecia areata represents an autoimmune condition where the body’s immune system mistakenly attacks healthy hair follicles, causing hair to fall out in patches. Its unpredictable nature poses significant challenges for treatment, underscoring the need for ongoing research and innovative approaches.</div>
<div></div>
<h3>Telogen Effluvium: Stress-Induced Hair Loss</h3>
<div>Telogen effluvium is a temporary form of hair loss triggered by significant stress, illness, or hormonal changes. It typically resolves within 16 weeks, as hair gradually returns to its normal growth cycle. Addressing the underlying cause is key to overcoming this condition.</div>
