## Understanding Probiotics and Their Benefits
![A person holding a bottle of probiotic supplement with a label showing 10 different probiotic strains](/uploads/content/A-person-holding-a-bottle-of-probiotic-supplement-with-a-label-showing-10-different-probiotic-strains-1024x777.jpg)
The World Health Organization defines probiotics as "live microorganisms that, when taken in adequate amounts, provide a health benefit to the host". This is a valuable way for people to maintain or improve their health. These beneficial bacteria are found in various foods and dietary supplements and play a crucial role in maintaining a healthy gut microbiome – the complex community of microorganisms that inhabit our own digestive tract and urinary tract.
By supporting the growth of good bacteria and inhibiting harmful bacteria, probiotics can help promote digestive health healthy digestion, immunity, and overall well-being.
### What are probiotics?
![A woman holding a cheap probiotic that contains lactobacillus acidophilus, lactobacillus rhamnosus, and lactobacillus plantarum](/uploads/content/A-woman-holding-a-cheap-probiotic-that-contains-lactobacillus-acidophilus-lactobacillus-rhamnosus-and-lactobacillus-plantarum-808x1024.jpg)
Probiotics can be found in certain fermented foods, such as yogurt and fermented vegetables, as well as dietary supplements. They come in various bacterial strains though, with Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, and Saccharomyces boulardii being the most well-researched and widely used.
When selecting a probiotic supplement, it's important to consider the number of colony forming units (CFUs), which indicates the quantity of live and active microorganisms in each serving. A therapeutic dose can range from 0.5 billion to 450 billion CFUs, depending on the specific strain.
Another factor to consider formulated probiotics is the presence of prebiotics, which are fibers that serve as nourishment for probiotic bacteria, thus enhancing their absorption and efficacy. Additionally, third-party certification can provide assurance that the product's quality, purity, potency, and ingredients have been tested and verified.
### Health benefits of probiotics
Research suggests that probiotics may offer a range of health benefits, including improved digestive and gastrointestinal health, immune health, skin health, mental health, and heart health. They may also aid in the management of gastrointestinal conditions, such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and inflammatory bowel disease (IBD).
Some probiotic supplements are specifically designed for certain populations, such as older adults, who may benefit from a product containing 30 billion CFUs of Bifidobacteria strains and key Lactobacilli strains.
This type of supplement may enhance physical strength, concentration, and emotional well-being in older individuals. For skin health, clinical studies have shown that certain probiotic supplements can help improve skin smoothness, firmness, and hydration.
For those seeking further immune system support, a subscription-based probiotic supplement provides a convenient daily dose and has been found to be particularly effective in enhancing immunity. As with any supplement, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before starting a new probiotic regimen to ensure safety and efficacy.

### CFUs (Colony Forming Units)
Colony Forming Units, or CFUs, are used in microbiology to estimate the number of viable cells of bacteria or fungi in a sample. In the context of probiotics, CFUs refer to the number of alive and active microorganisms in one serving of a probiotic dietary supplement. To achieve a desired effect, the amount of bacteria required varies depending on the supplement form specific strain.
A product listing the CFUs at the end of its shelf life indicates that it contains a therapeutic number of CFUs upon purchase. This is an important factor to consider when selecting a cost-effective probiotic supplement, as it ensures that you're getting a product with a sufficient number of live microorganisms to provide the desired health benefits.
### Strain diversity
Strain diversity refers to the variety of strains within a particular probiotic supplement. Different strains of probiotics can offer varying health benefits, so it's essential to choose a supplement with a diverse range of strains to ensure optimal efficacy.
When selecting a probiotic supplement, review the label to determine the specific probiotic strains included. This will help you find a supplement with the appropriate probiotic strain diversity to suit your specific health needs and goals.
### Shelf life and storage
Shelf life is the period during which a product can be stored without becoming unsuitable for use, consumption, or sale. This is a critical factor to consider when selecting a cost-effective probiotic supplement, as it can influence the potency and efficacy of the product.
When choosing a probiotic supplement, it's important to take into account the product's shelf life and storage requirements. For example, some probiotic supplements may require refrigeration to maintain their potency, while others are shelf-stable and can be stored at room temperature. Ensuring proper storage will help maintain the supplement's effectiveness throughout its shelf life.
