            <h2 class="text-3xl font-black text-gray-900 mb-6">What Gut Health Actually Means</h2>
            <div class="mb-6">
                <p class="mb-4">When we talk about "gut health," we're referring to more than just comfortable digestion. True gut health encompasses three interconnected systems:</p>
                <ul class="space-y-3">
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check-circle text-accent mt-1 mr-3"></i> <span><strong>Gut Barrier Integrity:</strong> The physical and chemical barrier lining your intestines that controls what gets absorbed into your bloodstream. A "leaky gut" allows toxins, undigested food particles, and bacteria to enter the body, triggering inflammation and immune responses.</span></li>
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check-circle text-accent mt-1 mr-3"></i> <span><strong>Microbiome Diversity:</strong> The variety and balance of bacterial species in your gut. Higher diversity is linked to better immune function, metabolic health, and disease resistance. Modern lifestyles often deplete this diversity through processed foods, antibiotics, and environmental factors.</span></li>
                    <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check-circle text-accent mt-1 mr-3"></i> <span><strong>Digestive Function:</strong> The mechanical and enzymatic processes of breaking down food, absorbing nutrients, and eliminating waste. Optimal digestive function requires the right pH, enzyme production, and motility.</span></li>
                </ul>
