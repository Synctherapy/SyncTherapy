## Frequently Asked Questions
How much water does a reverse osmosis system waste?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_12\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Wastewater ratios have improved dramatically. Older systems could waste 5-10 gallons for every gallon of pure water. Modern, efficient systems have ratios closer to 1:1 or 2:1 (waste to pure). Tankless systems are generally the most efficient in this regard.
Can I connect my RO system to my refrigerator or ice maker?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_13\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Yes, most RO systems can be connected to a refrigerator and/or ice maker. This typically requires an extra connection kit, which is often sold by the manufacturer. It's a great way to get purified, great-tasting water and ice.
Will an RO system lower my water pressure?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_14\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
The RO system itself operates on your home's water pressure, but it does not lower the pressure at your main faucets. The purified water comes out of a dedicated, smaller RO faucet. The flow rate from this faucet will be less than your main tap, but a properly functioning system (especially tankless models) should provide a steady, usable stream.
