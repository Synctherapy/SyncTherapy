## \\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_43\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_ Red Light Therapy Bed Cost: FAQs
How much does a NovoTHOR red light therapy bed cost?

The NovoTHOR standard model costs approximately $120,000-$130,000, while the NovoTHOR XL runs $130,000-$150,000. This includes delivery but may require additional electrical installation for 220V power. NovoTHOR is the most expensive red light bed because it's the only one with published clinical trials and is used by professional sports teams including the NFL, NBA, and UFC.
How much does red light therapy cost per session?

Red light therapy sessions cost $25-$200 depending on location. Average pricing: $50-$55 at wellness centers, $75-$150 at med spas, $25-$40 at tanning salons. Planet Fitness Black Card members get unlimited access to the Total Body Enhancement booth for $24.99/month. For chronic conditions requiring 36+ sessions, [home panels](/at-home-red-light-therapy/) often provide better value.
What is the cheapest commercial red light therapy bed?

The [Mito Red MitoRecharge](/mito-red-light-therapy-review/) at approximately $23,000 (full system) or $12,000 (bottom piece only) is the most affordable commercial-grade option. It runs on standard 120V power, includes 4,200 LEDs with therapeutic wavelengths (630nm, 660nm, 810nm, 850nm), and requires no special electrical installation. The Prism Light Pad system at $19,990 (two pads) offers another entry-level commercial option.
Is buying a red light therapy bed worth it?

A $50,000 bed at $50/session can generate $100,000+ annual profit with a 6-month breakeven. \\\*\\\*For home users:\\\*\\\* Usually no—[panels deliver the same therapeutic benefits](/best-red-light-therapy-panels/) at 5-10% of the cost. The main advantage of beds is convenience and treatment speed, not effectiveness.

What does Planet Fitness red light therapy cost?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_49\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Planet Fitness red light therapy (Total Body Enhancement/Beauty Angel booth) is included with the Black Card membership at $24.99/month. This provides unlimited 12-minute sessions. However, note that the Planet Fitness booth only uses red light wavelengths (620-700nm) without near-infrared (810-850nm), which limits its effectiveness for muscle and joint benefits. It's best for skin-related goals.
Why is red light therapy so expensive?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_50\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
Red light therapy beds are expensive due to: (1) High-quality medical-grade LEDs—17,000-45,000 per bed at $0.50-$2.00 each, (2) FDA Class II clearance requiring expensive clinical trials, (3) Precise wavelength calibration with binned LEDs, (4) USA manufacturing costs, and (5) R&D investment for published research. Budget beds cut corners on LED quality and skip clinical testing.
Are red light therapy beds FDA approved?
\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_PRESERVED\\\\_JSX\\\\_BLOCK\\\\_51\\\\_\\\\_\\\\_
The FDA doesn't "approve" devices—it "clears" them. Key distinctions: \\\*\\\*FDA Class II Medical Device (cleared)\\\*\\\* means clinical testing proved specific health claims (NovoTHOR, TheraLight have this). \\\*\\\*FDA Registered\\\*\\\* simply means the manufacturer submitted paperwork—no testing required. Many cheaper beds claim "FDA registered" which has minimal meaning. Always ask for FDA 510(k) clearance documentation for medical claims. Learn more in our [FDA devices guide](/fda-approved-red-light-therapy-devices-for-pain/).

For home use, most users are better served by [high-quality panels](/at-home-red-light-therapy/) rather than beds. If you specifically want a bed, the Mito Red MitoRecharge bottom piece ($12,000) or MitoPOD (portable full-body mat) offer the best value. Avoid cheap "home beds" under $5,000—most are retrofitted tanning beds with inadequate LED specifications.
