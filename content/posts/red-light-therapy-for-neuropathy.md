## The Pathophysiology of Neuropathy

Neuropathy occurs when the peripheral nerves are damaged. This can arise from metabolic conditions (like diabetic neuropathy), physical trauma (crush injuries or severing), autoimmune diseases, or neurotoxic drug exposure.

When a nerve is damaged, the myelin sheath (the protective coating around the nerve) breaks down, and the tiny blood vessels (vasa nervorum) that feed the nerve die incredibly quickly. Without an immediate blood supply bringing oxygen and nutrients, the nerve fiber slowly suffocates. 

This causes the sharp, burning, electrical "misfires" and the eventual numbness patients experience.
