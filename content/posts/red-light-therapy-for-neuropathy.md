<section id="the-root-problem">
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-6">The Root Problem: Micro-Vascular Starvation</h2>
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">To understand how to fix the nerve, you must understand why it died in the first place.</p>
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">Neuropathy (especially diabetic peripheral neuropathy) is fundamentally a blood flow problem. High blood sugar and systemic inflammation destroy the microscopic blood vessels (capillaries) that supply oxygen and nutrients to the very tips of your peripheral nerves in your feet and hands.</p>
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700 font-semibold text-accent">Without a blood supply, the nerve starves, suffocates, and begins to die. That death signals the brain with blinding, burning pain.</p>
</section>
