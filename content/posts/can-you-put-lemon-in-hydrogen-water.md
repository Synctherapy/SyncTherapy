<section id="rule-1-generator-safety">
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-6">Rule #1: Never Put Anything But Pure Water Inside the Generator</h2>
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">To understand why putting lemon inside the machine is catastrophic, you need to understand how hydrogen generation works at a chemical level.</p>
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">Premium hydrogen generators use a technology called <strong>SPE (Solid Polymer Electrolyte)</strong> and <strong>PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane)</strong>. At the base of the bottle are extremely sensitive, platinum-coated titanium plates. These plates run an electrical current through the water to split H₂O molecules into pure hydrogen and oxygen gas.</p>
    
    <h3 class="text-2xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-4">The Dangers of Adding Lemon Inside the Bottle</h3>
    <ul class="space-y-3 list-disc list-inside text-gray-800 mb-6 bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-gray-100">
        <li><strong>Corrosion of the Plates:</strong> The high citric acid content of the lemon will eat away at the delicate platinum coating on the electrodes.</li>
        <li><strong>PEM Membrane Fouling:</strong> The sugars, pulps, and minerals will instantly clog the nano-pores of the Proton Exchange Membrane.</li>
        <li><strong>Creation of Toxic Gasses:</strong> When you run an electrical current (electrolysis) through saltwater or electrolyte powder containing chlorides, the chemical reaction will literally produce <strong>chlorine gas</strong>, which dissolves into your drinking water.</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500 p-6 rounded-r-lg shadow-sm my-8">
        <p class="text-red-800 font-bold text-lg mb-2"><i class="fas fa-exclamation-triangle mr-2"></i>Clinical Nutrition Rule</p>
        <p class="text-red-700">The ONLY liquid that should ever touch the inside of your hydrogen generator is pure filtered, reverse osmosis, or distilled water. Never add flavors before generation.</p>
    </div>
</section>

<section id="does-acid-destroy-hydrogen">
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold mb-6">Does the Lemon Acid Destroy the Hydrogen Gas?</h2>
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">This is a brilliant chemical question. Does the high acidity (pH of ~2.5) of lemon juice neutralize or destroy the molecular hydrogen (H₂) gas?</p>
    
    <p class="text-2xl font-bold text-accent mb-6 py-2">No, it does not.</p>
    
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">Molecular hydrogen is an incredibly stable, uncharged gas. It is neither acidic nor alkaline, and its clinical efficacy is entirely independent of the water's pH. This is why <a href="/kangen-water-vs-hydrogen-water/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">alkaline "Kangen" water claims are largely marketing hype</a>.</p>
    
    <p class="mb-4 text-gray-700">Adding acidic lemon juice will lower the pH of the water, which is fantastic for stimulating your stomach's natural digestive fire. The dissolved hydrogen gas will remain suspended in the fluid, unaffected by the acidity, ready to be absorbed rapidly into your bloodstream to combat systemic inflammation.</p>
</section>
