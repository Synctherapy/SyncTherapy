faqs:
  - question: What are the signs of an unhealthy gut?
    answer: Common signs include bloating, gas, constipation, diarrhea, food intolerances, fatigue, skin issues like acne or eczema, and frequent illness. Persistent digestive discomfort is often the first indicator that your gut microbiome needs support.
  - question: How long does it take to improve gut health?
    answer: Most people notice improvements within 2-4 weeks of dietary changes and probiotic supplementation. However, fully rebalancing the microbiome can take 3-6 months of consistent effort depending on the severity of dysbiosis.
  - question: Should I take probiotics or prebiotics first?
    answer: Ideally, take both together as a synbiotic. Prebiotics feed the beneficial bacteria (probiotics), making them more effective. If starting one at a time, begin with probiotics and add prebiotics after 1-2 weeks.
  - question: Can gut health affect mental health?
    answer: "Yes. The gut-brain axis is a well-established connection. About 90% of serotonin is produced in the gut. Research shows that improving gut health through probiotics and diet can positively impact mood, anxiety, and cognitive function."
  - question: What is the best probiotic strain for gut health?
    answer: There is no single best strain — it depends on your goals. Lactobacillus acidophilus and Bifidobacterium lactis are well-researched for general digestive health. Saccharomyces boulardii is excellent for antibiotic-associated issues. Soil-based organisms like Bacillus subtilis offer unique benefits for immune function.
