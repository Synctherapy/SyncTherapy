<p>Pulsed electromagnetic field (PEMF) therapy has gained attention in recent years for its potential health benefits. Some people worry that PEMF might cause cancer, but this concern is not supported by current scientific evidence. <strong>Research suggests that PEMF therapy does not cause cancer and may even have anticancer effects in some cases.</strong></p>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76jf-uyzzm.jpg" alt="A laboratory setting with electromagnetic fields and cancer cells under observation"></p>
<p>Studies have looked at how PEMF affects different types of cancer cells. For example, <a href="https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/15330338221124658">PEMF therapy has been tested on breast and lung cancer cells</a> to see if it stops them from growing. Other research has checked if PEMF can make cancer drugs work better. In one study, <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8972140/">PEMF helped a drug called etoposide kill more breast cancer cells</a>.</p>

<h2>Understanding Pulsed Electromagnetic Fields (PEMF)</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76jq-eofs8.jpg" alt="A laboratory setup with a PEMF device emitting electromagnetic fields, surrounded by scientific equipment and research materials"></p>
<p>PEMF therapy uses magnetic fields to help the body heal. It works by sending pulses into the body. These pulses can affect cells and tissues in different ways.</p>
<h3>History and Development of PEMF Therapy</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy has roots in the early 20th century. Doctors first used it to help bones heal faster. In the 1970s, the FDA approved <a wpil="url" old="aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuc3luY3RoZXJhcHkuY2EvYmxvZ3MvcGVtZi10aGVyYXB5L2Jlc3QtcGVtZi1tYXRz" href="https://synctherapylocal.local/best-pemf-mats/">PEMF devices</a> for treating bone issues.</p>
<p>Over time, scientists found more uses for PEMF. They learned it could help with pain and swelling. Research showed it might also boost cell health.</p>

<h3>Principles of Bioelectromagnetics in PEMF</h3>
<p><a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cam4.861">Bioelectromagnetics</a> is key to how PEMF works. It looks at how electric and magnetic fields affect living things. PEMF uses this idea to change how cells work.</p>
<p>PEMF devices create weak magnetic fields. These fields can pass through the body easily. They cause tiny electric currents in tissues. These currents can change how cells act.</p>
<p>The effects depend on the strength and pattern of the pulses. Different settings can lead to different results. Some may reduce pain, while others might speed up healing.</p>
<h3>PEMF Devices and Their Applications</h3>
<p><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5992548/">PEMF devices</a> come in many forms. Some are large machines used in clinics. Others are small enough to use at home. They can be mats, pads, or even wearable items.</p>
<p>These devices are used for various health issues:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bone healing</li>
<li>Pain relief</li>
<li>Wound healing</li>
<li>Improving circulation</li>
<li>Reducing inflammation</li>
</ul>
<p>Some doctors use PEMF to help treat depression. Others use it for sports injuries. Research is also looking at how PEMF might help with cancer treatment.</p>
<p>PEMF therapy is generally safe. But people with certain medical devices, like pacemakers, should not use it. Always talk to a doctor before trying PEMF therapy.</p>

<h3>Utilizing PEMF Alongside Chemotherapy and Radiotherapy</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy may boost the effectiveness of chemotherapy and radiotherapy. It could help these treatments work better while reducing side effects.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">One study found</a> that PEMF therapy slowed tumor growth when combined with standard treatments. This suggests it could be a valuable addition to cancer care plans.</p>
<p>PEMF might make cancer cells more sensitive to chemotherapy drugs. It could also help protect healthy cells from radiation damage during radiotherapy.</p>
<p>More research is needed to fully understand how PEMF interacts with these treatments. But early results are promising for improving cancer therapy outcomes.</p>
<h3>Emerging Studies on PEMF as a Non-Invasive Anti-Cancer Strategy</h3>
<p><a href="https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0072944">Recent research</a> has explored using PEMF as a standalone cancer treatment. Some studies show that specific PEMF frequencies may selectively target cancer cells.</p>
<p>In one experiment, low-intensity PEMF impaired breast cancer cell growth. It did this without harming healthy cells. This suggests PEMF could offer a targeted approach to cancer treatment.</p>
<p>PEMF therapy is non-invasive and has few side effects. This makes it an attractive option for cancer patients who can't tolerate harsh treatments.</p>
<p>More clinical trials are needed to confirm PEMF's anti-cancer effects. But early results point to its potential as a new tool in cancer care.</p>

Studies have explored PEMF effects on various cancer types, examining impacts on cell behavior and potential treatment applications.</p>
<h3>Effects of PEMF on Cancer Cell Lines and Tumor Growth</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy has shown varied effects on different cancer cell lines. In breast cancer, <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cam4.861">PEMF exposure reduced proliferation</a> of MDA-MB-231 and MCF-7 cells. For glioblastoma, PEMF treatment decreased tumor cell viability.</p>
<p>Some studies found PEMF slowed tumor growth in animal models. However, results differ based on cancer type, PEMF parameters, and study design.</p>
<p>PEMF impact on gene expression in cancer cells has also been examined. Changes in genes related to cell division, death, and tumor suppression were observed in some cases.</p>
<h3>In Vivo and In Vitro Studies of PEMF Impact</h3>
<p>In vitro studies allow detailed analysis of PEMF effects on isolated cancer cells. These have shown changes in cell proliferation rates, viability, and death in various cancer lines.</p>
<p>In vivo research provides insights into PEMF impact on tumors in living organisms. Some animal studies reported reduced tumor size or growth rate with PEMF treatment.</p>
<p>Both approaches have strengths and limitations. In vitro work offers precise control, while in vivo studies capture complex biological interactions.</p>
<h3>Potential Mechanisms: Apoptosis, Angiogenesis, and Others</h3>
<p>PEMF may <a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">trigger apoptosis (programmed cell death) in some cancer cells</a>. This could help eliminate tumor cells without harming healthy tissue.</p>
<p>Effects on angiogenesis (formation of new blood vessels) have been studied. Some research suggests PEMF might reduce tumor blood supply, potentially limiting growth.</p>
<p>PEMF impact on cell senescence and autophagy in cancer cells is another area of investigation. These processes affect cell survival and could influence tumor progression.</p>
<p>Changes in cancer cell metabolism and energy production after PEMF exposure have also been observed in some studies.</p>

<p>Scientists look at data from different labs and countries. They use math to find patterns in the results.</p>
<p>Key steps in meta-analysis:</p>
<ul>
<li>Collect all relevant studies</li>
<li>Check study quality</li>
<li>Combine data</li>
<li>Look for overall trends</li>
</ul>
<p>This method helps spot small effects that single studies might miss. It also shows if results are the same across different tests.</p>

<h2>Safety and Potential Risks of PEMF</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76lf-ybvi9.jpg" alt="A person using a PEMF device with a cancer warning symbol in the background"></p>
<p>PEMF therapy has been studied for its effects on health and potential risks. Research has examined its relationship to cancer, long-term safety, and overall risk profile. Some studies suggest that while PEMF therapy may offer various health benefits, it is important to consider the <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/pemf-therapy-side-effects/">potential side effects of PEMF therapy</a>, such as skin irritation or headaches in some individuals. Further investigation is needed to fully understand these risks and to determine the long-term safety of this treatment modality. Continued research will help to clarify its efficacy and inform patients and healthcare providers about its appropriate use.</p>
<h3>Investigating the Relationship Between PEMF and Carcinogenesis</h3>
<p><a data-wpil="url" data-wpil-url-old="aHR0cDovL3BlbWYuc2cvd3AtY29udGVudC91cGxvYWRzLzIwMTgvMDcvU2NpZW5jZS1vZi1QRU1GLURyLi1HYXJyeS1GLi1Hb3Jkb24tR1JJLTIwMTIucGRm" href="https://pemf.sg/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Science-of-PEMF-Dr.-Garry-F.-Gordon-GRI-2012.pdf">Studies on PEMF therapy</a> have looked at its effects on cell behavior. Some research suggests PEMF may influence cell voltage, which differs between healthy and cancer cells.</p>
<p>Cancer cells typically have lower voltage compared to normal cells. This has led to questions about whether PEMF could impact cancer growth.</p>
<p>However, the evidence is mixed. Some studies indicate PEMF may inhibit cancer, while others show no effect.</p>
<p>More research is needed to fully understand how PEMF interacts with cancer cells. Scientists continue to investigate if PEMF could promote or hinder cancer development.</p>

<p>Experts recommend consulting a doctor before starting PEMF therapy. This is especially important for those with a history of cancer.</p>
<h3>Assessment of Long-Term Safety in PEMF Therapy</h3>
<p>Long-term safety of PEMF therapy is an ongoing area of study. <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/7909506/">Research on extended PEMF use</a> is still limited.</p>
<p>Some studies suggest PEMF is safe for long-term use in certain conditions. But more data is needed on effects over many years.</p>
<p>Key considerations for long-term PEMF safety:</p>
<p>• Dosage and frequency of treatments
• Type of PEMF device used
• Individual health status
• Potential interactions with medications</p>

<h2>Comparative Analysis of PEMF and Other Cancer Treatments</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76ma-h0vl6.jpg" alt="A laboratory setting with various medical equipment and devices, including a PEMF machine, alongside other cancer treatment options"></p>
<p>PEMF therapy is one of many approaches used to treat cancer. It differs from traditional methods in key ways. PEMF may work well with other treatments to boost their effects. There are also other alternative options to consider.</p>
<h3>Traditional Vs. Electromagnetic Modalities</h3>
<p>Surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy are common cancer treatments. PEMF uses electromagnetic fields instead. Unlike surgery, PEMF is non-invasive. It doesn't require cutting into the body.</p>
<p>Radiation therapy uses high-energy beams to kill cancer cells. PEMF uses lower energy fields. This may cause less damage to healthy tissue.</p>
<p>Chemotherapy drugs travel through the whole body. PEMF can be applied to specific areas. This focused approach may have fewer side effects.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/15368378.2024.2345606">Studies suggest</a> PEMF could slow cancer growth. But more research is needed to fully compare its effects to traditional treatments.</p>
<h3>Synergistic Effects with Existing Cancer Therapies</h3>
<p>PEMF may work well with other cancer treatments. It could make them more effective.</p>
<p>Some research shows PEMF can make cancer cells more sensitive to radiation. This could allow for lower radiation doses.</p>
<p>PEMF might also boost the effects of chemotherapy drugs. It may help the drugs enter cancer cells more easily.</p>
<p>Hormone therapies change hormone levels to slow cancer growth. PEMF could potentially enhance these effects. But more studies are needed.</p>
<p><a href="https://sciendo.com/article/10.1515/raon-2016-0014">Combining PEMF with drugs</a> like cisplatin increased tumor-fighting effects in mice. This suggests PEMF could improve outcomes when used with other treatments.</p>
<h3>Alternative Treatment Options and Efficacy</h3>
<p>Besides PEMF, there are other new cancer treatments being studied.</p>
<p>Senolytic drugs target old or damaged cells. These cells can promote cancer growth. Removing them may slow tumor progression.</p>
<p>Radionuclide therapy uses radioactive drugs to kill cancer cells. It can be very precise, like PEMF.</p>
<p>Photodynamic therapy (PDT) uses light to activate cancer-killing drugs. Photothermal therapy (PTT) uses heat from light to destroy tumors.</p>
<p>These newer options show promise. But like PEMF, they need more research to prove their effectiveness compared to standard treatments.</p>
<p>It's important to discuss all treatment options with a doctor. They can help determine the best approach for each person's specific case.</p>

<p>PEMF could be used to <a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">boost the immune system</a> in cancer patients. This might help the body fight cancer cells better.</p>
<p>Another area to explore is using PEMF to make cancer cells more sensitive to treatment. This could make other therapies work better.</p>

<p>PEMF could be part of a <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5992548/">personalized treatment plan</a>. This plan would include other therapies that work well with PEMF.</p>

<h3>Principles of Bioelectromagnetics in PEMF</h3>
<p><a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cam4.861">Bioelectromagnetics</a> is key to how PEMF works. It looks at how electric and magnetic fields affect living things. PEMF uses this idea to change how cells work.</p>
<p>PEMF devices create weak magnetic fields. These fields can pass through the body easily. They cause tiny electric currents in tissues. These currents can change how cells act.</p>
<p>The effects depend on the strength and pattern of the pulses. Different settings can lead to different results. Some may reduce pain, while others might speed up healing.</p>

<p>These devices are used for various health issues:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bone healing</li>
<li>Pain relief</li>
<li>Wound healing</li>
<li>Improving circulation</li>
<li>Reducing inflammation</li>
</ul>
<p>Some doctors use PEMF to help treat depression. Others use it for sports injuries. Research is also looking at how PEMF might help with cancer treatment.</p>
<p>PEMF therapy is generally safe. But people with certain medical devices, like pacemakers, should not use it. Always talk to a doctor before trying PEMF therapy.</p>

<p>In one experiment, low-intensity PEMF impaired breast cancer cell growth. It did this without harming healthy cells. This suggests PEMF could offer a targeted approach to cancer treatment.</p>
<p>PEMF therapy is non-invasive and has few side effects. This makes it an attractive option for cancer patients who can't tolerate harsh treatments.</p>
<p>More clinical trials are needed to confirm PEMF's anti-cancer effects. But early results point to its potential as a new tool in cancer care.</p>

<h3>Effects of PEMF on Cancer Cell Lines and Tumor Growth</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy has shown varied effects on different cancer cell lines. In breast cancer, <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cam4.861">PEMF exposure reduced proliferation</a> of MDA-MB-231 and MCF-7 cells. For glioblastoma, PEMF treatment decreased tumor cell viability.</p>

<p>PEMF impact on gene expression in cancer cells has also been examined. Changes in genes related to cell division, death, and tumor suppression were observed in some cases.</p>
<h3>In Vivo and In Vitro Studies of PEMF Impact</h3>
<p>In vitro studies allow detailed analysis of PEMF effects on isolated cancer cells. These have shown changes in cell proliferation rates, viability, and death in various cancer lines.</p>
<p>In vivo research provides insights into PEMF impact on tumors in living organisms. Some animal studies reported reduced tumor size or growth rate with PEMF treatment.</p>
<p>Both approaches have strengths and limitations. In vitro work offers precise control, while in vivo studies capture complex biological interactions.</p>
<h3>Potential Mechanisms: Apoptosis, Angiogenesis, and Others</h3>
<p>PEMF may <a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">trigger apoptosis (programmed cell death) in some cancer cells</a>. This could help eliminate tumor cells without harming healthy tissue.</p>
<p>Effects on angiogenesis (formation of new blood vessels) have been studied. Some research suggests PEMF might reduce tumor blood supply, potentially limiting growth.</p>
<p>PEMF impact on cell senescence and autophagy in cancer cells is another area of investigation. These processes affect cell survival and could influence tumor progression.</p>
<p>Changes in cancer cell metabolism and energy production after PEMF exposure have also been observed in some studies.</p>

<p>Key considerations for long-term PEMF safety:</p>

• Type of PEMF device used
• Individual health status
• Potential interactions with medications</p>
<p>Regular check-ups are advised for those using PEMF long-term. This allows doctors to monitor for any changes or side effects.</p>

<h2>Comparative Analysis of PEMF and Other Cancer Treatments</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76ma-h0vl6.jpg" alt="A laboratory setting with various medical equipment and devices, including a PEMF machine, alongside other cancer treatment options"></p>
<p>PEMF therapy is one of many approaches used to treat cancer. It differs from traditional methods in key ways. PEMF may work well with other treatments to boost their effects. There are also other alternative options to consider.</p>
<h3>Traditional Vs. Electromagnetic Modalities</h3>
<p>Surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy are common cancer treatments. PEMF uses electromagnetic fields instead. Unlike surgery, PEMF is non-invasive. It doesn't require cutting into the body.</p>
<p>Radiation therapy uses high-energy beams to kill cancer cells. PEMF uses lower energy fields. This may cause less damage to healthy tissue.</p>
<p>Chemotherapy drugs travel through the whole body. PEMF can be applied to specific areas. This focused approach may have fewer side effects.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/15368378.2024.2345606">Studies suggest</a> PEMF could slow cancer growth. But more research is needed to fully compare its effects to traditional treatments.</p>
<h3>Synergistic Effects with Existing Cancer Therapies</h3>
<p>PEMF may work well with other cancer treatments. It could make them more effective.</p>
<p>Some research shows PEMF can make cancer cells more sensitive to radiation. This could allow for lower radiation doses.</p>
<p>PEMF might also boost the effects of chemotherapy drugs. It may help the drugs enter cancer cells more easily.</p>
<p>Hormone therapies change hormone levels to slow cancer growth. PEMF could potentially enhance these effects. But more studies are needed.</p>
<p><a href="https://sciendo.com/article/10.1515/raon-2016-0014">Combining PEMF with drugs</a> like cisplatin increased tumor-fighting effects in mice. This suggests PEMF could improve outcomes when used with other treatments.</p>
<h3>Alternative Treatment Options and Efficacy</h3>
<p>Besides PEMF, there are other new cancer treatments being studied.</p>
<p>Senolytic drugs target old or damaged cells. These cells can promote cancer growth. Removing them may slow tumor progression.</p>
<p>Radionuclide therapy uses radioactive drugs to kill cancer cells. It can be very precise, like PEMF.</p>
<p>Photodynamic therapy (PDT) uses light to activate cancer-killing drugs. Photothermal therapy (PTT) uses heat from light to destroy tumors.</p>
<p>These newer options show promise. But like PEMF, they need more research to prove their effectiveness compared to standard treatments.</p>
<p>It's important to discuss all treatment options with a doctor. They can help determine the best approach for each person's specific case.</p>

<h2>Considerations for Future Research</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76mx-svzjd.jpg" alt="A laboratory setting with scientific equipment and a PEMF device, surrounded by researchers conducting experiments and analyzing data"></p>
<p>More research is needed to fully understand the relationship between PEMF and cancer. Scientists should focus on filling knowledge gaps, exploring new uses in cancer care, and tailoring treatments to individual patients.</p>
<h3>Addressing Gaps in Current PEMF Cancer Research</h3>
<p>Studies on PEMF and cancer have shown mixed results. More large-scale trials are needed to confirm if PEMF helps or harms cancer patients.</p>
<p>Researchers should look at different types of cancer and treatment settings. They need to figure out the best PEMF settings for each situation.</p>
<p>Long-term studies are important too. These will show if PEMF has any effects years after treatment ends.</p>
<p>Scientists should also study how PEMF interacts with other cancer treatments. This includes chemotherapy, radiation, and immunotherapy.</p>
<h3>Prospects for New Applications in Oncology</h3>
<p>PEMF may have new uses in cancer care beyond direct treatment. It could help manage side effects from other therapies.</p>
<p>Some studies suggest PEMF might reduce pain or swelling in cancer patients. It may also help with fatigue or sleep problems.</p>
<p>PEMF could be used to <a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">boost the immune system</a> in cancer patients. This might help the body fight cancer cells better.</p>
<p>Another area to explore is using PEMF to make cancer cells more sensitive to treatment. This could make other therapies work better.</p>
<h3>Implications for Personalized Medicine</h3>
<p>Each person's cancer is unique. PEMF treatments could be tailored to fit each patient's needs.</p>
<p>Doctors might use genetic tests to see who would benefit most from PEMF. They could adjust the treatment based on a patient's specific cancer type.</p>
<p>PEMF could be part of a <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5992548/">personalized treatment plan</a>. This plan would include other therapies that work well with PEMF.</p>
<p>Researchers should study how factors like age, health status, and cancer stage affect PEMF results. This will help create more effective treatments for each person.</p>

<h2>Conclusion and Outlook</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76ni-8qzxz.jpg" alt="A laboratory setting with scientific equipment and researchers studying the effects of PEMF on cells"></p>
<p>PEMF therapy shows promise as a potential cancer treatment. <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cam4.861">Studies have examined its effects on different cancer cell lines</a>. The research suggests PEMF may help fight cancer in some cases.</p>
<p>More clinical trials are needed to fully understand PEMF's impact on cancer. <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cam4.861">Only a few studies have tested PEMF therapy in cancer patients so far</a>. Larger, long-term trials will provide clearer answers.</p>
<p>Future research will likely focus on optimal PEMF parameters for cancer therapy. <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/VIW.20220029">Scientists are studying factors like field strength and pulse frequency</a>. This may help create more effective PEMF treatments.</p>
<p>PEMF could potentially work alongside other cancer therapies. Researchers may explore combining PEMF with chemotherapy or radiation. This might boost overall treatment success.</p>
<p>As studies continue, PEMF's role in cancer care may expand. It could become a useful tool for doctors and patients. But more evidence is needed before drawing firm conclusions.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<p><img src="https://synctherapylocal.local/wp-content/uploads/content/v2-o76nu-hzn2i.jpg" alt="A person using a PEMF device near a cancer research lab"></p>
<p>PEMF therapy raises important questions about its effects on various types of cancer and potential interactions with other treatments. Safety considerations and impacts on specific cancer types are key areas of interest.</p>
<h3>What are the implications of PEMF therapy on breast cancer treatment?</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy shows promise for breast cancer treatment. Some studies suggest it may <a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">slow cancer cell growth</a> and enhance the effects of chemotherapy drugs. More research is needed to fully understand its role.</p>
<h3>How does PEMF therapy interact with chemotherapy?</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy may improve chemotherapy effectiveness. It can potentially increase drug uptake by cancer cells. Some research indicates PEMF may also help reduce chemotherapy side effects.</p>
<h3>Can PEMF therapy influence the growth of brain cancer cells?</h3>
<p>Early studies show PEMF therapy may affect brain cancer cells. It appears to slow the growth of certain types of brain tumors in laboratory settings. More clinical trials are needed to confirm these effects in patients.</p>
<h3>Are there any side effects associated with PEMF therapy in cancer patients?</h3>
<p>PEMF therapy is generally considered safe for cancer patients. Minor side effects like mild discomfort or tingling sensations may occur. Patients should consult their doctor before starting PEMF therapy.</p>
<h3>What impact does PEMF therapy have on pancreatic cancer?</h3>
<p>Research on PEMF therapy for pancreatic cancer is limited. Some studies suggest it may help reduce <a data-wpil="url" href="https://synctherapylocal.local/hydrogen-water-cancer/">tumor growth</a> and improve chemotherapy effectiveness. More clinical trials are needed in this area.</p>
<h3>How does exposure to magnetic fields affect the progression of cancer?</h3>
<p>The effects of magnetic fields on cancer progression are complex. Some studies indicate certain frequencies may <a href="https://www.scirp.org/html/2-8902709_82944.htm">slow cancer growth</a>, while others show no effect. The strength and duration of exposure are important factors to consider.</p>
