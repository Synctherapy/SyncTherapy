<h2 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1724456598556">Fibromyalgia Overview</h2>
<div class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small margin-top_none" section-id="1724456598557"><br></div>
<div class="bggle_video bggle--block text__center margin_vertical--small margin-top_none " section-id="1724456598558"><iframe allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/v9FsxdSuZ4Q" style="aspect-ratio: 16 / 9; width: 100%" title="v9FsxdSuZ4Q"></iframe></div>
<div class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small margin-top_none" section-id="1724456598559">
<br><p>Fibromyalgia is a chronic condition marked by widespread pain, fatigue, and cognitive difficulties. It affects an estimated 2-4% of the population, with a higher prevalence among women.</p>
<br>
</div>
<h3 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1724456598562">Symptoms of Fibromyalgia</h3>
<div class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small margin-top_none" section-id="1724456598563">
<br><p>The primary symptom of fibromyalgia is a <strong>persistent and widespread pain</strong> throughout the body, often described as a deep aching or burning sensation. Individuals may also experience:</p>
<br><p></p>
<ul>
<li>Tenderness or sensitivity in multiple areas</li>
<li>
<strong>Fatigue</strong>, even after sleep</li>
<li>Cognitive difficulties, commonly known as “fibro fog,” which includes problems with memory, concentration, and organization</li>
</ul>
<br>
</div>
<h3 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1724456598568">Causes and Risk Factors</h3>
<div class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small margin-top_none" section-id="1724456598569">
<br><p>The exact causes of fibromyalgia remain unknown. However, several factors may increase the risk of developing the condition:</p>
<br><p></p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Genetic predisposition</strong>: Family history of fibromyalgia</li>
<li>
<strong>Infections</strong>: Some illnesses appear to act as a trigger</li>
<li>
<strong>Physical or emotional trauma</strong>: Stressful or traumatic events, like car accidents, can sometimes initiate <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/conquering-hip-pain-a-fibromyalgia-warriors-guide/"  data-wpil-monitor-id="7905">fibromyalgia symptoms</a></li>
<li>
<strong>Gender</strong>: Women are diagnosed with fibromyalgia more often than men</li>
</ul>
<br>
</div>

<h3 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1724456598612">Potential Side Effects</h3>
<div class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small margin-top_none" section-id="1724456598613">
<br><p>PEMF therapy is generally considered to be a <strong>safe</strong> treatment option for fibromyalgia, with few reported side effects.</p>
<br><p>From the research, side effects are typically mild and could include temporary discomfort, fatigue, and irritability.</p>
<br><p>A study examining the <em>therapeutic effects of <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/best-pemf-mats/">whole-body devices applying pulsed electromagnetic fields</a></em> highlighted minimal side effects for fibromyalgia patients, supporting its safety in therapeutic strategies.</p>

<h3 class="no--margin-vertical margin-top_none bggle_title bggle--block " section-id="1724456598620">Who Should Avoid PEMF</h3>
<div class="bggle--block bggle_text margin_vertical--small margin-top_none" section-id="1724456598621">
<br><p>Certain individuals should be cautious or avoid PEMF therapy:</p>
<br><p></p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>People with electronic implants</strong>: such as pacemakers or insulin pumps, since PEMF can interfere with the function of these devices.</li>
<li>
<strong>Pregnant women</strong>: there is not enough research to confirm the safety of PEMF therapy during pregnancy.</li>
<li>
<strong>Those with an active hemorrhage</strong>: PEMF therapy may exacerbate the condition.</li>
</ul>

<br><p><strong>How does PEMF therapy work for fibromyalgia?</strong><br>
PEMF therapy works by sending low-level electromagnetic fields into the body to reduce pain and inflammation. </p>
<br><p>Studies have shown that patients with fibromyalgia may experience an <a href="https://journals.lww.com/clinicalpain/fulltext/2009/10000/Fibromyalgia_pain__do_we_know_the_source_.00011.aspx">improvement in their overall symptoms</a> after receiving PEMF treatment.</p>

PEMF therapy is generally considered safe when protocols are followed correctly. Side effects are typically rare and mild.</p>
<br><p><strong>How long does a PEMF therapy session last?</strong><br>
A typical session can last anywhere between 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the protocol recommended.</p>
<br><p><strong>How many PEMF sessions are needed for fibromyalgia relief?</strong><br>
The number of sessions varies by individual and the severity of symptoms. </p>
<br><p>Some studies suggest a 12-week treatment plan may be beneficial for those with fibromyalgia, but treatment plans should be tailored to the individual.</p>
<br><p><strong>Can PEMF therapy be used at home?</strong><br>
Yes, there are PEMF devices available for home use, but they should be used under the guidance of a healthcare provider.</p>
