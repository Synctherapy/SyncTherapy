## Fibromyalgia Overview
Fibromyalgia is a chronic condition marked by widespread pain, fatigue, and cognitive difficulties. It affects an estimated 2-4% of the population, with a higher prevalence among women.
### Symptoms of Fibromyalgia
The primary symptom of fibromyalgia is a \\\*\\\*persistent and widespread pain\\\*\\\* throughout the body, often described as a deep aching or burning sensation. Individuals may also experience:
\\\* Tenderness or sensitivity in multiple areas
\\\* \\\*\\\*Fatigue\\\*\\\*, even after sleep
\\\* Cognitive difficulties, commonly known as “fibro fog,” which includes problems with memory, concentration, and organization
### Causes and Risk Factors
The exact causes of fibromyalgia remain unknown. However, several factors may increase the risk of developing the condition:
\\\* \\\*\\\*Genetic predisposition\\\*\\\*: Family history of fibromyalgia
\\\* \\\*\\\*Infections\\\*\\\*: Some illnesses appear to act as a trigger
\\\* \\\*\\\*Physical or emotional trauma\\\*\\\*: Stressful or traumatic events, like car accidents, can sometimes initiate [fibromyalgia symptoms](/red-light-therapy-for-arthritis/)
\\\* \\\*\\\*Gender\\\*\\\*: Women are diagnosed with fibromyalgia more often than men
