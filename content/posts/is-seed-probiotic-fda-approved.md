            <h2 class="text-3xl font-extrabold text-gray-900 mb-6 border-b pb-4">FDA Approval vs. FDA Regulation: What's the Difference?</h2>
            <p class="mb-6 text-lg text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">This is one of the most misunderstood aspects of the supplement industry. Understanding the difference will help you make better decisions about any supplement you consider.</p>
            
            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8">
                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-lg border-t-4 border-red-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4"><i class="fas fa-prescription-bottle text-red-500 mr-2"></i>FDA Approval (Drugs)</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Before a pharmaceutical drug can be marketed in the United States, it must go through <strong>pre-market approval</strong> by the FDA. This involves:</p>
                    <ul class="mt-4 space-y-2 text-gray-700">
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-times text-red-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> Extensive clinical trials (often 10+ years)</li>
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-times text-red-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> Proven safety and efficacy data</li>
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-times text-red-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> Review by the FDA's Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER)</li>
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-times text-red-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> FDA inspectors visit manufacturing facilities</li>
                    </ul>
                    <div class="mt-4 bg-red-50 p-3 rounded-lg">
                        <p class="text-sm text-gray-700 font-semibold">Cost: $1-2 billion+ and 10-15 years per drug</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl shadow-lg border-t-4 border-green-500">
                    <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4"><i class="fas fa-capsules text-green-500 mr-2"></i>FDA Regulation (Supplements)</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700">Dietary supplements are regulated under the <strong>Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA)</strong> of 1994, which works differently:</p>
                    <ul class="mt-4 space-y-2 text-gray-700">
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> Supplements do NOT require pre-approval</li>
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> Manufacturers are responsible for safety</li>
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> FDA can take action against unsafe products</li>
                        <li class="flex items-start"><i class="fas fa-check text-green-500 mt-1 mr-2"></i> Must follow Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)</li>
                    </ul>
                    <div class="mt-4 bg-green-50 p-3 rounded-lg">

                    <h3 class="font-bold text-xl text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-question-circle text-primary mr-2"></i>Why don't probiotic supplements get FDA approval?</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">The Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA) of 1994 created a separate regulatory category for dietary supplements. Under DSHEA, supplements are treated more like food than drugs — they don't require pre-market approval because they're not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease. This is why supplement companies cannot make drug-like claims.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl border border-gray-200 shadow-sm hover:shadow-md transition-shadow">
                    <h3 class="font-bold text-xl text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-question-circle text-primary mr-2"></i>What does "FDA registered" mean?</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">"FDA registered" means the manufacturing facility has registered with the FDA and is subject to inspection. It does NOT mean the FDA has approved the product. Registered facilities must follow cGMP (Current Good Manufacturing Practices) and can be inspected at any time by the FDA.</p>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl border border-gray-200 shadow-sm hover:shadow-md transition-shadow">
                    <h3 class="font-bold text-xl text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-question-circle text-primary mr-2"></i>Can I trust supplement claims?</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">Be skeptical of products that make medical claims or promise dramatic results. Look for: third-party testing certifications, published clinical trials, transparent ingredient lists, and companies that provide Certificate of Analysis. Seed checks all these boxes.</p>

                <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-xl border border-gray-200 shadow-sm hover:shadow-md transition-shadow">
                    <h3 class="font-bold text-xl text-gray-900 mb-3"><i class="fas fa-question-circle text-primary mr-2"></i>What is Eurofins testing?</h3>
                    <p class="text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">Eurofins Scientific is one of the world's largest laboratory testing networks. When a product is "Eurofins verified," it means the batch has been tested by a third-party lab for potency, purity, and identity — independent verification that goes above and beyond basic FDA requirements.</p>
