            <section id="how-it-works">
                <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold">How Does Red Light Therapy Actually Work?</h2>
                <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-md space-y-4 text-gray-700">
                    <p>To understand why Kala devices are effective, it helps to know the science behind them. Red light therapy, also known as <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/photobiomodulation-explained/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold" target="_blank">photobiomodulation (PBM)</a>, uses specific, clinically-proven wavelengths of light to stimulate your body's natural healing processes. The foundational mechanism is its interaction with mitochondria, the energy generators within our cells.</p>
                    <p>The primary molecule that absorbs this light is <strong>Cytochrome C Oxidase (CcO)</strong>, a key enzyme in our cells' energy production line. Under stress from injury or inflammation, a molecule called nitric oxide (NO) can block CcO, drastically reducing cellular energy (ATP) production. According to a study in <a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5523874/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold" target="_blank">PMC</a>, photons from red and NIR light are absorbed by CcO, breaking the bond with nitric oxide. This single event restores energy production and releases NO, which then acts as a vasodilator to improve local blood flow.</p>
                    <p>Kala devices utilize some of the most researched wavelengths:</p>
                    <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2 pl-4">
                        <li><strong>Red Light (633-660nm):</strong> This wavelength is primarily absorbed by the skin, making it highly effective for improving skin health, stimulating collagen, and accelerating the healing of surface <a href="https://synctherapylocal.local/red-light-therapy-and-scars/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold" target="_blank">scars</a>.</li>
                        <li><strong>Near-Infrared Light (830-850nm):</strong> This longer wavelength penetrates deeper into tissues, muscles, and joints. It's ideal for reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and enhancing muscle recovery.</li>
                         <li><strong>Blue Light (465nm):</strong> Found in the Kala Mask, this wavelength is clinically proven to kill acne-causing bacteria by targeting porphyrins they produce, as noted in a <a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3352636/" class="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold" target="_blank">comparative study</a> on acne treatments.</li>
                    </ul>
                     <p>By combining these wavelengths, Kala devices offer a comprehensive treatment that addresses issues from the skin's surface to the deep tissues beneath.</p>
