<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Understanding Arthritis: What You're Actually Dealing With</h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">The Reality of Arthritis in Our Community</h3>



<p>Arthritis affects over 6 million Canadians, and here in Greater Victoria, it's one of the most common reasons people seek treatment at clinics like mine. But "arthritis" isn't just one condition—it's actually an umbrella term for over 100 different types of joint disease.</p>



<p>The two most common types I see are:</p>



<p><strong>Osteoarthritis (OA):</strong> The "wear and tear" arthritis that develops when the protective cartilage cushioning your joints gradually breaks down. It typically affects weight-bearing joints like knees, hips, and the spine.</p>



<p><strong>Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA):</strong> An autoimmune condition where your immune system mistakenly attacks the lining of your joints, causing inflammation, pain, and potential joint damage. Unlike OA, RA often affects joints symmetrically (both hands, both knees).</p>



<p>Other forms include psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, and juvenile arthritis—each requiring specialized assessment and treatment.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">How Arthritis Impacts Your Daily Life</h3>



<p>The reality of arthritis extends far beyond joint pain. My patients describe:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Morning stiffness that can last 30 minutes to several hours</li>



<li>Reduced range of motion that makes simple tasks challenging</li>



<li>Fatigue that affects energy levels throughout the day</li>



<li>Difficulty with stairs, particularly here in Victoria's hillier neighbourhoods</li>



<li>Trouble with fine motor tasks like opening jars or buttoning shirts</li>



<li>Hesitation to commit to social activities due to unpredictable symptoms</li>
</ul>



<p>This isn't just about physical limitations—it affects your independence, your social connections, and your mental health. That's why effective arthritis care requires more than just treating the joint itself.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>



<p><strong>Q: Do I need a referral to see you at Sync Therapy?</strong></p>



<p>No. Massage therapists and athletic therapists don't require referrals in BC. You can book directly, and many patients start treatment with me while waiting for specialist appointments.</p>



<p><strong>Q: Will massage therapy cure my arthritis?</strong></p>



<p>Arthritis has no cure, but massage therapy and athletic therapy can significantly improve your pain, mobility, and quality of life. We focus on managing symptoms and optimizing function.</p>



<p><strong>Q: How is athletic therapy different from physiotherapy?</strong></p>



<p>Both are excellent options. Athletic therapists specialize in musculoskeletal injury assessment and rehabilitation with a strong focus on functional movement. The main practical difference is that physiotherapy is more commonly covered by extended health plans, though many plans now cover athletic therapy as well.</p>



<p><strong>Q: Can nutrition really make a difference with arthritis?</strong></p>



<p>Yes. Research consistently shows that anti-inflammatory dietary patterns can reduce inflammatory markers and symptoms in arthritis patients. It's not a replacement for medical treatment, but it's a powerful complement.</p>



<p><strong>Q: How long will I need treatment?</strong></p>



<p>Arthritis is typically a chronic condition, so ongoing management is common. However, treatment frequency evolves—you might start with weekly sessions, then move to bi-weekly, then monthly maintenance, or come in during flare-ups.</p>



<p><strong>Q: What if I can't exercise because of pain?</strong></p>



<p>That's exactly why you need professional guidance. I design programs specifically for where you're at right now—starting with gentle range of motion and gradually building strength as your pain decreases.</p>



<p><strong>Q: Do you accept insurance?</strong></p>



<p>Yes. We direct bill to most major insurance providers for massage therapy services. Athletic therapy and nutrition services are submitted through your extended health benefits, and we provide detailed receipts for reimbursement.</p>
